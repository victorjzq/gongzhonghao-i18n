# Reading Bars: Trend Identification and Small Account Growth Strategies

**Market Trends**

Accurately identifying trends requires observation across different timeframes. Taking the daily and hourly charts as an example, each trading day has 7 hours of trading, producing 7 hourly bars.

*   If most of these bars are bear bars with large bodies, while bull bars are few and lack momentum, this typically indicates strong downward pressure, insufficient upward momentum, and an overall bearish trend.

Mastering this simple observation method allows you to quickly determine market direction.

* * *

**Keep Your Strategy Simple to Improve Execution**

Trading is like driving a vehicle -- bars are the vehicle, and the moving average is the track. Only when the track is clear can the trading direction continue smoothly.

Once a trend is clear, trading with the trend typically offers an advantage. Overly complex strategies often backfire.

* * *

How Can Small Accounts Grow Effectively?

**High-Frequency Trading Is Key**:

*   Small account growth depends on a higher number of trades; only high-frequency trading can accelerate compound growth.

*   As the number of trades increases, the win rate per trade typically converges toward 50%: in a small number of trades, you might achieve a 70% win rate, but the more trades you make, the overall win rate will gravitate toward 50%.

**The Core of Improving High-Frequency Trading Win Rate**:

*   The key is having a mechanism to control trading rhythm.

*   Be clear about when to increase position size and trade frequently, and when to reduce position size and trade frequently, pursuing overall optimal reward-to-risk.

* * *

**Common Mistake: Trend Following + Blind Averaging Down**

Many traders tend to enter the market with a small position and add to their position when facing losses to average down. However, this approach often leads to larger losses. Blindly averaging down is not a wise choice.

* * *

**Precisely Adjust Position Size to Improve Trading Efficiency**

**How to Identify When to Increase or Decrease Position Size?**

*   When market odds are unfavorable, proactively reduce position size to control risk.

*   When the market shows clear favorable odds, decisively increase position size to capture higher returns.

Observing market index movements often directly impacts trading odds. Being skilled at observing index changes helps adjust trading position size more scientifically.
