# Price Action: Microchannels (Part 5)

When the market is rallying strongly and price is far from the moving average, you will see pullbacks and second legs. Therefore, you can counter-trade short on the first downward breakout.

If you do not want to go short on the first breakout, you can wait for more trend-following trades to appear and then look for a retest of the trap price level.

The scenario where the first breakout is more likely to succeed is during consecutive extreme moves — specifically three consecutive extreme moves. So you would expect at least one deeper pullback. In this type of situation, we often get two legs back up to the moving average or back to the last high.

Determining whether the first breakout will succeed is critical. In cases where you are betting on the first breakout succeeding, if going long, it is best to have a bull signal bar; if going short, it is best to have a bear signal bar. Do not just buy above any bear bar, especially late in a trend — wait for a good signal bar before entering.

The reason microchannels form is that enough traders on the prior bar believe price will continue in the current direction. For instance, when we see a two-bar breakout, the third bar appears because most people believe the first two bars will lead to a third; the fourth bar appears because people believe the first three bars will lead to a fourth. Therefore, waiting only for the microchannel to end before trading usually means missing many opportunities. In many cases, we see multiple consecutive bars in the same direction, and if you only trade after the microchannel breaks, you may be too late due to the completion of the internal structure, and you will also miss many otherwise reasonable trading opportunities.

Therefore, the concept of "betting on a failed breakout" is not just about waiting for the breakout to actually fail before trading. It is about understanding that when the probability of the breakout failing is very high, you can use this expectation to protect yourself and trade more aggressively. This is something Al frequently discusses in the trading room. Over the past few years, he has repeatedly emphasized: in certain situations, bulls can confidently buy at highs because they know that if price drops below the prior bar, there will be buying support below.

So when bulls believe price can easily accelerate upward, they aggressively buy in advance and then add below a bar. In this case, you do not necessarily have to wait until the breakout fails to trade — you can also use the expectation that "the breakout will very likely fail" to employ more aggressive entry methods.

Ranked from highest urgency to lowest urgency:
Highest urgency: Buy at the close in a high-momentum environment, because we are in the early phase of a new market cycle, having just broken out strongly from a trading range. Bar bodies are large with little overlap, showing breakout and continuation characteristics, and price will very likely continue higher. In this situation, both momentum and market context show aggressive buying, and traders should also buy aggressively — buy at the close.

Lowest urgency: The market may already be in the late stage of a leg, or in a trading range environment. At this point, you need to wait more patiently for a lower buying opportunity.
