# Price Action: Limit Orders and Market Orders

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 2 years. Today is my 145th article. You should also study Price Action.

What every trader should do is patiently wait for a trend to appear.

Stop orders and limit orders are actually two opposing ways of placing bets.
For example, if a bull wants to bet the market will go up, he will place a buy stop order above the high of a bar. That bar's high is lower than the previous bar's, indicating a pullback is in progress. If price rises and breaks above the high of that bar, his order will be triggered for entry. He is betting that after the breakout, price will continue to rise and form a new trend.

Stop orders are used to bet on trends. If the market truly continues trending upward, he will make money.
When the market is in a trend, traders who use stop orders are more likely to profit. Conversely, the success rate for limit orders is much lower.

But every trade must have a counterparty.
When a bull buys with a stop order above a certain high, someone must be selling. That seller might be using a limit order, or a market order. Limit order traders typically set a price during a market rally -- for example, selling at the high of the previous bar -- betting that this rally will not continue and will instead reverse.

In other words, limit orders are betting against the trend.
When the market is rising and you are selling, you are betting it will not go much further and will soon reverse downward, allowing you to exit at a lower price for a profit. Many scalpers who use limit orders will continue to scale in when price moves against them, continuing to bet that the market will come back.

When the market is in a trend, stop order traders have more opportunities and find it easier to make money, because betting on the trend is more consistent with the current market environment during a trend.
But in a trading range, it is different.
A trading range has no clear direction; it frequently goes up and down. Therefore, limit order traders actually have more opportunities, because this type of repeatedly reversing market favors limit order reversal bets.

Again, for every trade, someone is doing the opposite of what you are doing.
For example, when a bear sells with a limit order, the counterparty might be a bull buying with a stop order.
Similarly, when bears are bearish, they can also sell with stop orders, placed below the low of the previous bar. They are betting price will break below that low and continue to decline.

If you see many bars with long tails, and price action whips up and down without consecutive strong bull bars or bear bars, it means the market is in a trading range.
On such days, price fluctuates back and forth without a clear direction.

If you want to make money from trading and earn a living, the most likely path to success is swing trading using stop orders for entry. Especially in a trading range, you should look for upward reversal signals in the bottom third, particularly the second signal, which tends to be more reliable.

Every trader must be aware of two things:
First, you are trying to make money -- you are here to earn a living.
Second, it has to be interesting to you.
There are many ways to make money, but not every method is right for you.
If you do not enjoy a particular method, it will be very hard to stick with it long term.
Some people prefer limit orders, some prefer scalping, and some prefer swing trading.
As long as you can make money and you enjoy it, you are doing it right.
The most important thing is finding a trading style that matches your personality.

For most people, looking for trends, swing trading, and entering with stop orders is the easiest way to make money.

When you see large bear bars closing at their lows, if two or more of these bars appear consecutively, it usually means you should consider closing your position.
These bars have very little tail, they are all solid bear bars, and they all close at the low, indicating the bears are pressing hard.
Especially when these bars break below a key level, forming a valid breakout, it usually means a trend is starting.
At this point, the market condition is shifting from a trading range to a trend.

Most traders, seeing this kind of change, should close their long positions.
Of course, there are scalpers who use very wide stops, and even in a trading range they dare to scale in. If managed properly, they can also make money. But that is not the approach for most people.

If you are a beginner, it is best not to trade frequently within a trading range, especially not to scalp with limit orders.
Wait until you have more experience and a larger account, and then consider scalping -- it will be safer.
Until then, the better approach is: patiently wait for a trend, look for a clear breakout, and then enter with a stop order in the direction of the trend to swing trade.
