# Price Action: Approaches to Swing Trading

## 1. Two Approaches to Always In

**Approach One: Trade Every Reversal**

Suitable for short-term, high-frequency traders, characterized by flexibly following the market rhythm.

For example:

*   Go long above Bar 1;

*   Continue buying at Bar 2's close;

*   Reverse to go short after Bar 11 breaks down;

*   Close the position at Bar 34;

*   Go long at Bar 52, go short at Bar 64...


Every time the direction switches, try to capture a small swing.

**Approach Two: Only Trade Key Reversals**

Suitable for swing traders, focusing on major trends and reducing frequent operations.

For example:

*   Go short after Bar 11 breaks down;

*   Reverse to go long when Bar 68 is broken out.


Stay still in between, patiently waiting for high-quality opportunities.

**Note: Bar 34 and 52 are not high-quality reversal points; Bar 68 is the one with clear structural confirmation.**

* * *

### 2. The Worst Mistake: Taking the Middle Road

Hesitating between two styles is the root cause of losses.

Example (wrong approach):

*   Go short at Bar 11;

*   Get scared out at Bar 16;

*   Go short again at Bar 29;

*   Get stopped out again at Bar 34;

*   Go short again at Bar 49;

*   Go long above Bar 52...


This kind of chaotic entry and exit, without a clear strategy, only leads to "buying high and selling low," losing money repeatedly.

**Recommendation:**

*   Either only trade key reversal points;

*   Or trade every reversal;

*   But never alternate between aggressive and conservative approaches, wavering back and forth.


* * *

### 3. Handling a Short Entry at Bar 50

**If it is a swing short position, there are two choices:**

1.  Place the stop loss above Bar 48 and leave it there;

2.  If you don't want to endure the pullback, then exit decisively when Bar 52 is broken.


However, **the area around Bar 55 is the midpoint of the bearish breakout from Bars 48-50**, and this position should be a zone for bears to add to their position, not a place to close.

**Conclusion:**

*   Exit logic must be clear and definitive;

*   Either stop out early, or hold all the way through;

*   Hesitating while stopping out easily causes you to miss the main trend.
