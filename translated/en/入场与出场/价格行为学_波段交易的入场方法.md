# Price Action: Entry Methods for Swing Trading

The market has inertia and tends to continue its existing behavior.
You always need to look left
to anticipate what will happen on the right.

After three or four consecutive bull bars closing near their highs,
traders can enter long at any time, for whatever reason.
Some traders will buy and then scalp out,
continuing to buy and repeatedly scalp;
other traders will buy once and hold to the end;
still others will buy once first,
then scale in at one or two additional buy points,
adding to the position in the direction of the trend (scaling in).

A "pullback" refers to a bar whose low
drops below the low of the previous bar.

If you see consecutive strong trend bars moving in the same direction,
the probability of the market continuing in that direction is higher.

Bears who are "Always In short" will mostly exit at the third or fourth bull bar,
and most traders will view this as an "Always In long" signal,
expecting at least a "two-leg" rally:
first leg, pullback, second leg.

If three bull bars form, some traders will buy.
In most cases, traders will wait for four bull bars
before buying at the close to go long,
placing the stop loss below the lowest point,
and holding the position until the market deteriorates significantly.
