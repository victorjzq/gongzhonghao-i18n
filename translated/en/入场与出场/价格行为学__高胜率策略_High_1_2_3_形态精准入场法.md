# Price Action: [High Win-Rate Strategy] High 1, 2, 3 Pattern Precision Entry Method

1. Trading Logic Prerequisites
========

In a clear bull trend, the core method for identifying pullback entry points is the **High 1, 2, 3** pattern (in a bear trend, the same logic applies and is called Low 1, 2, 3).

*   **Pullback Definition**:

*   The current bar's low is lower than the previous bar's low.



2. Entry Signal Confirmation Methods

(1) Standard Pattern: High 1, 2, 3 Pattern Definitions

*   **High 1**:

*   In a strong bull trend, when a bar's high is equal to or slightly lower than the previous bar's high, and the next bar breaks above this high, it forms an H1 bull flag (H1 pullback does not require the low to break below the prior low).

*   **High 2**:

*   A second pullback leg appears in the trend. The pullback depth may increase, but the trend structure remains intact.

*   **High 3**:

*   A three-leg pullback in the trend (e.g., a converging wedge or triangle). The third leg typically makes a lower low before reversing.

(2) Strong Trend Pattern: High 1 Special Cases

*   **High 1 is only applicable in strong trends** (e.g., consecutive large bull bars).
*   If the trend weakens or enters a trading range, it is better to wait for a High 2 or High 3 for confirmation.



3. Detailed Execution Rules

*   **High 1 Occurrence Frequency**:

*   If 5 consecutive High 1 patterns appear in a strong trend, it may indicate the trend is entering its final stage.

*   **High 3 Stop Loss Placement**:

*   High 3 patterns typically come with a wider stop loss. Position size should be reduced accordingly to control total risk (e.g., enter with half position).

*   **When Price Fails to Break Above the Prior High as Expected**:

*   If the price fails to effectively break above the prior high after a High 2 or High 3 pullback, it may be evolving into a trading range. You should immediately set a stop loss or consider a reversal trade.

Practical Trading Summary

*   The stronger the trend, the simpler the pullback entry (High 1 is sufficient).
*   When the trend weakens or pullbacks deepen, more complex structures (High 2 or High 3) are needed for confirmation.
*   High 3 typically requires a wider stop loss — always pay attention to position size management.
*   Once a pullback structure fails to break above the prior high, cut losses decisively to avoid trading range risk.

Mastering the High 1, 2, 3 patterns will help you precisely identify trend pullback entry points and achieve long-term, stable trading profits.
