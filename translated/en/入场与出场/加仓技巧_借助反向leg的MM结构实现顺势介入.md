# Scaling In Techniques: Using the Measured Move Structure of Counter-Trend Legs to Enter With the Trend

In trend-following trading, **"scaling in"** is not about blindly chasing highs. Instead, it means waiting for the market to present a new structure before entering again with precision.
This article introduces a practical scaling in method: **Using the 1-hour timeframe counter-trend leg and measured move (MM) structure to find the ideal position for adding to a trend-following position.**

* * *

1. Strategy Logic: Trade With the Larger Trend, Against the Smaller — The Rhythm Point of Institutional Entry
--------------------

Once the daily trend is clearly established, the market often produces short-term pullbacks on smaller timeframes.
These short-term "counter-trend legs" are precisely the windows where the dominant trend force gathers momentum.

The point where a counter-trend leg ends is typically the take-profit zone of the prior counter-trend traders,
and at the same time, it is the **high win-rate entry point** for the dominant trend force to re-enter.

* * *

2. Execution Details: Four-Step Method
------------

### 1. Confirm the Major Trend on the Daily Chart

*   First, determine whether the daily chart shows a clear trend (e.g., a major rally / major decline);

*   If the trend is unclear, **do not use this method** to avoid getting trapped in a trading range.

### 2. Identify the Counter-Trend Leg on the 1-Hour Timeframe

*   Within the trend, observe whether the 1-hour chart shows **a pullback swing opposite to the main trend**;

*   Ensure the leg is complete, with a clear starting point and natural convergence.

### 3. Calculate the MM Target (Measured Move)

*   Draw the counter-trend leg length from "starting point to ending point";

*   Copy that distance and **project it forward** to obtain the MM target level.

*   This is the level where the market may complete its counter-trend movement and prepare to resume the main trend.

### 4. Precise Scaling In Execution

*   **First add-on entry**

    : Place a limit order at the 1x MM level;

*   **Second add-on entry**

    (optional): Place another limit order at the 2x MM level;

*   Set the stop loss at the end of the counter-trend leg, then move it one tick beyond to ensure a buffer.

* * *

3. Understanding the Essence: The Bears' Take-Profit Zone Is the Bulls' Scaling In Zone
----------------------

*   After a counter-trend leg completes, bears typically take profit at 1R or 2R;

*   These two levels happen to be exactly where bulls can scale in with low risk, following the trend;

*   **The trend-following logic remains unchanged — you are simply waiting for a better entry position**.

**In other words:**

> **Where the bears' trade ends is where the bulls' attack begins.**

* * *



---

* * *

4. Summary
----

When the trend is clear, **do not chase rallies or rush in — wait for the pullback to end before re-entering**,
using the MM target of the counter-trend leg. Scaling in this way has both logical justification and structural support.

This is not "doubling down on a bet" — it is **the trend-follower's ambush strike**.
