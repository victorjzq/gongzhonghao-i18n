20 EMA Gap Bars — a bar whose **highest point does not touch the 20 EMA**

> **Highs of bars are below EMA in strong Bull trend**

> **70% chance selloff is bear trap**

After a bear trap, the market will most likely **retest the prior high.**

**When EMA Gap Bars appear during a bull trend, the trend often resumes**, but it has usually entered the **final push** of that trend leg.

The result of the prior high test is an important swing trading entry point. A failed test often forms an HH MTR (Higher High Major Trend Reversal).

**Every pattern simultaneously contains both a "success scenario" and a "failure scenario."**

But before an opposing signal bar actually appears,

you must **assume it is successful by default**.

Before a clear opposing signal bar appears,

you **cannot prematurely assume it will fail**.

True failure

only becomes valid **after the opposing structure has already appeared**.

Before that, it is merely **a success scenario that has not yet been invalidated**.

If there is a support level below, it is more likely to be a trap.
