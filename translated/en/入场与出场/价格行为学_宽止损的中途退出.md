# Price Action: Early Exit with a Wide Stop Loss

## 1. The Stop Loss Is Your Last Line of Defense

Many traders habitually set a "wide stop loss" and then do nothing else, thinking:

> "I've set my stop loss. I'll just hold until it gets hit."

This is the wrong approach.

**A stop loss is only your "disaster insurance"** —

1.  It protects you in case of a sudden power outage, fainting, or internet disconnection;

2.  It protects you from extreme market moves that could bankrupt you;

3.  It is there to protect your life, not to gamble with.

Your real exit should be based on **whether market behavior indicates that "you are wrong,"**
not "wait until the stop loss gets hit."

## 2. Several Clear Signals to "Admit You Are Wrong" Proactively

### 1. Three consecutive large bear bars (especially closing below midpoint)

For example:

*   Bar 4, 5, 6

*   Bar 16, 17, 18

This kind of price action represents **consecutive confirmation of bear control.**
Your original reason for going long has already become invalid.

At this point, you cannot wait for the stop loss — you must retreat proactively.

* * *

### 2. A bear bar closing near its low, appearing at the top of a channel

For example:

*   You bought at Bar 3;

*   Bar 4 closes at its low, near the upper boundary of the channel;

This is not "technical noise" — it is a structural failure.
Holding the position any longer will only turn a small loss into a big one.

* * *

### 3. A large outside bear bar breaking below the prior low with no bounce

The structure looks like:

*   Bar 64, 65 gradually decline;

*   Bar 67 produces a large outside bear bar.

This is the market's final hint to you: "Leave now, while you still can."

* * *

## 3. Don't Exit Only When Your Stop Is Hit — Exit When You Know You Are Wrong

> "I set a stop loss, but I never intend to let it actually get hit."

The stop loss is for "in case I pass out," not for "letting it decide whether I'm right or wrong."

What you need to do is:

*   Observe bar behavior;

*   Determine whether the original premise of your trade still holds;

*   If the market has **clearly negated it**, you must **act decisively and exit proactively**.

---

* * *

## 4. Summary in One Sentence:

**If you are swing trading, you should manage with a wide stop loss until Price Action goes against your expectation.**
--------------------------------------

If the trade is wrong, do not wait for it to become right.
