# Price Action: Losing Money Due to Mistakes (Part 4)

One of the main reasons beginners lose money
is that they do not know how to properly manage their trades.

To manage a trade correctly,
you must make decisions on three things:
position size, risk (stop loss placement), and reward (where to take profits).
If you get any one of these wrong,
you will very likely lose money.

Only about 10% of the bars on a chart are in a strong breakout state --
in other words, when betting on trend continuation is high probability.
The remaining 90% of the time, the market is in a bull channel, bear channel, or trading range.
All three appear on this chart.
Every trading range has a rally and a decline.

90% of bars are in a bull channel, bear channel, or trading range.
In this situation,
if you buy or sell at any given moment within the range,
you have about a 40% to 60% chance of profiting,
and a 40% to 60% probability that the market will soon move against you.

On every single bar on the chart,
if you buy, you have a 40% to 60% probability of making money.
If you sell, you have a 40% to 60% probability of losing money.

In other words, in a trading range market,
it is impossible to have a 70% probability trade.

According to the Traders' Equation,
you know that to make money,
you only need to ensure
at least a 40% probability of profiting.

As long as you buy or sell at any point within a channel or trading range,
and when you take profits
you capture at least twice your actual risk,
then you have a profitable strategy.

But the reason beginners lose money
is mostly still due to psychological issues and false beliefs.

For example, they believe perfect trades exist.
They also believe the market frequently moves randomly,
with no rational trading behavior behind it.
They also believe that low-probability events will save them from bad decisions made at wrong positions.
But none of these things can save them.

Once you realize you have made a mistake,
you should exit immediately.

Most traders lose money
simply because they fail to manage their trades properly.

Many times, they enter the market with a plan,
and they genuinely believe
they will follow that plan.

But when it comes time to actually make decisions,
they fail to execute,
because they lack the emotional control to do the right thing,
and the result is losing money.

90% of the time,
as long as you manage properly,
you can exit at breakeven, with a small loss,
or even with a profit.

Even bad trades,
if managed properly,
can be turned into winners.

You can buy at the top of a trading range and sell at the bottom,
and if managed properly,
you can avoid losses or even profit.

Let me emphasize again,
this is in 90% of market structures.

But if you sell into a strong breakout,
you will likely lose money,
because the market has a 70% probability of continuing upward.

Most of the time, the market's probability falls between 40% and 60%.

You are trading too large a position size to be able to execute your plan.
You care too much.
You care too much about losing money.
The result is that you cannot manage your trades properly.

If you are too sensitive to losses,
then do not take the trade.

If you cannot manage it properly,
wait for the next opportunity where you can manage it well before trading.
