# Step Away from the Screen

**AL:** The example I often give is, "after opening a position, go to the supermarket." Some beginners, after a string of losing trades, are desperate to "make it all back," so they close out at the first sign of a small profit while keeping their stop loss very wide — which, mathematically, almost guarantees losing money. My advice to beginners: first, find a reasonable-looking entry signal, determine your stop loss distance, then set your profit target at least twice the stop loss, and simply go to the supermarket and check the result when you come back. You'll find that sometimes your stop loss gets hit, but quite often you'll capture the entire move; statistically, this is far more consistently profitable than "taking small profits and running" every time.

Reflections:

*   After entering a trade, deliberately step away from the trading screen — try going to the supermarket, taking a walk, reading, or doing any other unrelated activity.

*   The purpose is to avoid constantly checking account fluctuations, reduce emotional interference caused by short-term price movements, and prevent impulsive decisions.

*   I prefer trading on the daily chart or higher timeframes, avoiding excessive obsession with short-term fluctuations, and cultivating a more calm and patient trading mindset.
