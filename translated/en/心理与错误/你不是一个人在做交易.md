# You Are Not Trading Alone

Over the past five years, I took many detours, only to realize that what truly works is Al Brooks' Price Action.

After leaving my job, I had more time, and I wanted to properly organize my trading notes. These writings are also a product of my own.

I chose trading because I found it free and sustainable. No matter what happens in the outside world, the market is always there, and it won't be easily replaced.

Beyond trading, writing is what makes me happiest.

The trading life is lonely:

Staring at charts, unable to decide,
Feeling terrible after a stop loss,
Having a bad streak for several days and starting to doubt yourself--
I've been through all of it.

I write these things not to teach you how to trade, nor to argue about who's right or wrong. I just want to tell you:

Someone is going through the same things as you.

The instrument you're watching, I'm trading it too,
The level you're staring at, I'm watching it too,
The emotions you're experiencing, I've experienced them as well.

What trading truly tests is not who's smarter, but who can be more consistent.

Don't panic when it drops, don't fear when you're wrong,
Just keep following your system.

Most traders are silent.

Every day, watching charts alone, reviewing trades alone, carrying emotions alone.

You must have had moments like these:

You followed your plan, yet still felt uneasy;
You took a stop loss according to the rules, yet still couldn't help blaming yourself;
You saw others making money, said nothing, but inside you were in turmoil.

Nobody truly understands how you feel.

Family can't comprehend it, friends can't relate, and the market won't give you a chance to explain.

The loneliest part isn't having no one to talk to,
But having no one to endure the volatility with you, no one to keep the rhythm with you.

So, I write a little something every day.

Not for you to trade by, but to remind you:

Was your trade today based on your system, or on gut feeling?
Was this loss a normal pullback, or something that needs serious reflection?
Right now, are you staying steady, or getting a bit anxious?

If you find that the structure, entry points, and rhythm I write about happen to match what you were thinking, then you know:

You are not watching the market alone, and you are not trading alone.

Your hesitation, self-blame, and regret--I've been through them all,
I just wrote them down one step ahead of you.

Loss is not the scariest thing.

What's truly scary is trading for so long that you start doubting yourself and losing your direction.

So, I hope I can show up once a day.

One sentence, one structure, one question worth thinking about.

Not much, but useful.

To help you stay a little clearer, a little steadier, and keep moving forward.

Not in the same place, yet resonating across a thousand miles, sharing a knowing smile.

April 11, 2025, written in Istanbul.
