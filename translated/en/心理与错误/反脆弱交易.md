# Antifragile Trading

This is my 121st day learning Price Action with Victorjia, and today's note is my 30th article. You should be learning Price Action too.

Any system can only endure if it conforms to the concept of antifragility, so your trading should also be an antifragile behavior.

How to define antifragile behavior:

You need to pay special attention to the dimension of time.

Things whose marginal returns increase over time conform to antifragile logic.

A trading system either becomes exposed to risk over time (fragile), or becomes exposed to opportunity over time (antifragile).

When you incorporate time as a variable, the core question becomes:

Does the system, over long periods of disturbance, **nonlinearly increase potential gains rather than potential losses**?

The concept of antifragility requires us to harness volatility.

To harness volatility, you need to design a structure.

You need to not seek stability, only survival.

Stable small wins are often an illusion created by suppressing volatility. True safety comes from structures that can survive chaos.

So in the vast majority of trading time, you experience small wins and small losses. True compounding comes from amplifying profits through uncertainty in extreme market conditions. Black swans are not risks--they are accelerators of compounding.

Specifically, how do we design an antifragile trading structure that harnesses volatility? We can start with position management.

Distributing across multiple positions is the most important thing. If you need to follow a trend, continuously adding to your position as the trend progresses is a fragile behavior. As the trend moves, your position size grows larger and larger. As your position grows larger, your resistance to volatility becomes weaker and weaker. If a spike occurs, your risk becomes enormous.

Here, the coordinate system of our antifragile system is this trade, or rather today's trading.

In today's trading, if I capture a trend, I first open a position to establish my holdings, then watch as the lows of this trend keep rising. I add to my position at each pullback's High 1--this seems very reasonable, right?

But this behavior is fragile. Not every trend eventually transitions into a trading range--what if it reverses directly?

When this trend extends for a long time, your position grows larger and larger, and you become more and more dangerous. Your risk is fully exposed, and you have no method to deal with black swan events, namely reversals or direct spikes.

So this behavior is actually fragile behavior.

Think about it: how can you make this trading pattern into an antifragile behavior?

In fact, the concept of adding to positions and individual trades is the same. We can design a pattern for taking profits on individual trades.

You can take 1R or 2R profits on each individual order, then continue opening new positions along the trend, following the market, using single positions to feel out how far the market can go.

This way, you naturally diversify your risk. For example, suppose you added to your position 5 times and lost everything on the last one. Compare this with the new pattern: the profits from the first 4 trades are all protected, and you only lose the last one when you exit. In reality, you still come out well ahead.

This pattern makes each trade an independent probe, without reusing fragile positions.

This is entirely consistent with Taleb's Barbell Strategy:

On one side is safety (small gains locked in by taking profits), and on the other side is openness (the next new position betting on uncertainty).

By **splitting the continuity of time and position**, each instance of volatility becomes an opportunity for "regeneration" rather than an "accumulation of risk."

This is essentially not resisting volatility, but harnessing volatility.
