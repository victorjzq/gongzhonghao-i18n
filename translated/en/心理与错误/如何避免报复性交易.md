# How to Avoid Revenge Trading

First, don't think this is only a beginner's problem

You've lost several trades in a row, and your mindset has crumbled.
That's completely normal. It's not just you.
**Even experienced traders can't withstand consecutive losses.**

Your edge is already very thin. A single stray thought can wipe it out entirely.
If you're feeling unwell today, your mind isn't clear, or your life is a mess,
Then you're not trading--you're banging your head against a wall.

* * *

**Second, accept that you are not a machine**

People have emotions.
Sometimes when your state isn't right, just don't trade.
Don't beat yourself up over missing an hour or a day of market action.

**Sometimes the best trade is to stop.**

* * *

**Third, the more you try to recover, the faster you die**

*   In tennis, some people start smashing the ball when they panic, and end up losing even worse.

*   In baseball, after three strikeouts, if you force a home run on the fourth at-bat, you'll just get struck out again.

*   In football, a quarterback who throws an interception and then tries a long pass will just get intercepted again.

Trading is the same. **When emotions take over, you make more mistakes, and losses pile up.**

* * *

**Fourth, what is revenge trading?**

You think: "I've already lost three trades in a row. Even if this one isn't the best opportunity,
as long as it goes up, I can win it all back in one shot!"

Sound familiar? That's revenge trading.

**The problem isn't whether you win or lose,**
but rather: **is this the kind of trade you would normally take?**

If not, then you're just gambling.

* * *

**Fifth, how do you prevent it? What should you do?**

It's simple:

1.  **Cut your position size first.** Once the pressure lightens, your mind clears up.

2.  **Allow yourself to sit out.** Not every bar needs your response.

3.  **Stop trying to break even.** Treat every trade as a fresh start.

4.  **Only trade setups you're familiar with.** Don't change strategies on the fly. Don't gamble.

5.  **Remember, you're here to manage risk,** not to pick a fight.

* * *

**Conclusion:**

Trading is not a fight.
You're not here to prove who's right or wrong.
You're just here to take the trade you're supposed to take.

See yourself clearly, let go of the market,
And you'll have the chance to survive long and live well.
