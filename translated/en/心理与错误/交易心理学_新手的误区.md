# Trading Psychology: Common Mistakes of Beginners

1.  Why You Shouldn't Focus on Profits
    Many beginners ask: how many points should I earn per day to be considered competent? This kind of thinking is the easiest way to lose focus. Watching points or dollar amounts often leads to missing quality opportunities, forcing trades that shouldn't be taken, and once losses occur, your mindset falls apart, leading to a chain of errors. The correct approach is to review whether the logic of each trade is sound, whether the entry and exit timing is appropriate, and whether there are any management mistakes.

2.  Trade Management Matters More Than Entry Precision
    In real markets, 90% of price action occurs within trading ranges or channels. In this environment, regardless of how precisely you enter, as long as your management is solid, profitability is possible. For example: you can sell at the low, then scale in at the high, and still exit with a profit. The key is not the "perfection" of the entry, but rather the subsequent position management, stop loss, and scaling in strategies.

3.  "Quickly Recovering Losses" Is a Trap
    After losing money, rushing to find the next opportunity to recover your losses often sends you into a deeper hole. Imperfection is the norm in trading. Calmly accepting losses, learning from them, and focusing your attention on the execution of the next trade — rather than rushing to win back points — is how you avoid cascading errors.

4.  How Many Points Do Professional Traders Make Per Day?
    Professional traders don't think about how much money they should make every day. They focus on execution and rules:
    Swing traders make a few trades per day, typically targeting about half the average daily range. If the average daily range is 12 points, they usually target 6 points.
    High-frequency scalpers enter and exit frequently, with targets that can reach 1.5 to 2 times the average daily range.
    But copying their point targets doesn't guarantee success. What you need to learn is their trade structure and consistent execution.

5.  How Do Professional Traders Think?
    They don't think "I need to make $3,000 today." Instead, they ask:
    Was each trade executed strictly according to the rules?
    Was the stop loss placed at a reasonable level?
    When the market deviates from expectations, how should I respond? Should I exit immediately, or observe a few more bars?
    Professional traders care about "right or wrong," not the profit or loss of a single trade.

6.  Embrace Losses, Embrace Uncertainty
    The market occasionally takes a low-probability path, and even correct judgments can result in losses. Accept that losses are part of trading — don't let emotions dictate your decisions. As long as you consistently make the right trades, long-term results will naturally turn favorable.

7.  Key Takeaways
    – Beginners should not focus on "how many points to earn each day" — focus on trade logic and management
    – Trade structure and position management are far more important than profit targets
    – 90% of price action is in trading ranges — management takes priority over entry precision
    – Losses are normal — don't rush to recover them
    – Professional traders prioritize rule execution over point targets
    The goal is not to earn a certain number of points, but to "execute every trade correctly" — profits are simply the byproduct of correct trading
