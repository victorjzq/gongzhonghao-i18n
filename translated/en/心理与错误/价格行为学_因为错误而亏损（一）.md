# Price Action: Losing Money Due to Mistakes (Part 1)

One of the common reasons beginners lose money is psychological factors and false beliefs.
Most of the money beginners lose is because of their incorrect understanding of the market.
They try to apply the logic and common sense from everyday life to trading,
but this logic simply does not work in trading.
If you are doing the same things as most people,
then you are very likely doing the wrong things.

Every trader, no matter how good, will have losing trades,
because some good trades will go bad.
No trade is ever 100% certain.
But the losses traders suffer from "making mistakes"
far exceed the losses from "good trades that went bad."
And the problem with beginners is: they are ignorant.

"Ignorant" simply means lacking information,
but in trading, lacking information means losing money.
Fortunately, this problem is not hard to fix --
it just takes time to learn,
and a willingness to accept some perspectives you would not normally consider.

Here are some common beliefs that cause beginners to lose money due to ignorance:

**The Perfect Trade**
Many beginners believe there exists some kind of "perfect entry" in the world,
and they even get angry at me for not telling them the so-called "perfect level."
But in reality, there is no perfect trade.
Every trade is a combination of **risk, reward, and probability**,
and you cannot have the optimal outcome for all three simultaneously.
A high-reward, low-risk trade
often means the probability of success is very low.
And a trade with a very high probability of success
often means its risk/reward ratio is poor and the profit is small.
The so-called perfection is just an illusion.

**Noise**
You may have heard the term "noise."
But this is a terrible concept.
It implies that the market sometimes moves randomly,
but the market is never random.
Even those small fluctuations you consider meaningless --
there is always someone trading them.
You may not want to participate,
but high-frequency trading firms, algorithmic trading systems, and institutional capital
are constantly trading these tiny movements.
They have determined it is a good price, so they are buying or selling.
Even if it is meaningless to you,
within their trading systems, this is a valuable, well-reasoned action.
So every single movement in the market is driven by someone,
and reflects certain people's trading intentions.

**Either Bulls or Bears Are Making Money**
This is another common misconception among beginners.
They always think the market can only let one side make money.
But in reality, 90% of the time,
as long as you use a wider stop loss, scale in reasonably, and manage properly,
both bulls and bears can make money.
Because the market spends most of its time in a trading range,
rather than in a one-directional trend,
the market gives both bulls and bears opportunities to profit.

In summary,
many of the losses beginners suffer stem from the false beliefs they bring with them,
and these beliefs often appear rational and common-sensical,
but they simply do not work in the market.
Trading is not an extension of everyday life --
it has its own independent rules and logic.
To survive, you must let go of the intuitions from life experience
and relearn and build a new way of thinking.
