# Find Your Own Rhythm

AL: Many people know Warren Buffett, but he has a partner named Charlie Munger. Munger once said he prefers someone with an IQ of 130 who thinks it's 110, over someone with an IQ of 150 who thinks it's 170. The implication is that you should be able to accept making mistakes rather than being overconfident. If you want to win through "fast thinking," you can't compete with high-frequency trading computers--they're far faster than us. But there are other ways to make money in the market. You can choose a timeframe that matches your own thinking speed, such as the daily chart or longer periods. "Slow thinking" can also be profitable. Or you can stick to stocks of companies with proven track records, buy when they've dropped by a certain amount, and hold for the long term. The logic isn't complicated, and it's entirely feasible. For short-term trading, even a 5-minute chart can generate a satisfying income. The key is to find a trading style that matches your personality and time rhythm--don't force yourself to chase a speed you can't keep up with.

Reflections:

## I. Being Clear-Headed Matters More Than Being Smart

*   The market never lacks smart people, but smart people are prone to overconfidence, and overconfident people are prone to mistakes.

*   Investing isn't an IQ competition. It's about being clear-headed enough to know yourself and only making money from what you truly understand.

---

## II. Don't Be Greedy--Only Take What You Can Grasp

*   The market's opportunities are infinite, but human energy is limited.

*   When the market is good, seize the opportunities that belong to you, take your profits, and don't be greedy or reckless.

---

## III. When the Market Is Uncertain, Decisively Step Aside

*   When you can't read the market, don't trade. Patiently wait for clearer opportunities.

*   Learning to pass on uncertain market conditions is the most effective form of risk management.

---

## IV. Choose a Trading Rhythm That Suits You

*   Personally, I prefer the daily timeframe, checking the market just once a day.

*   Futures, U.S. stocks, Hong Kong stocks, A-shares, crypto... anything tradable can be unified under the same rhythm.
