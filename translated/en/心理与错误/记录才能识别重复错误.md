# Only Through Record-Keeping Can You Identify Repeated Mistakes

**AL: The way human memory works is fascinating--the pain of the moment gradually fades, but you remember the event itself. I've made many foolish decisions and was in agony during losses, yet looking back, that sense of pain has already disappeared.**

Most traders in their exploratory phase will try every "wrong approach," repeating mistakes countless times, until the pain becomes unbearable and they finally reflect: I must stop this behavior. Trading often requires "counter-intuitive thinking"--for example, when the market has risen a lot, the safest approach is usually not to chase the rally, but to watch for the risk of a top reversal. This is the opposite of everyday life experience, which is why it takes longer to adapt.

**Reflections:**

* * *

## I. Pain Fades, but Experience Endures

*   Human memory tends to dilute the pain felt at the time, yet clearly remembers the event itself.

*   The pain from past trading losses was once excruciating, but over time that pain has gradually blurred, leaving behind clearer lessons.

## II. The Inevitable Path of a Trader: Mistakes and Reflection

*   Most traders in their early stages will try every "wrong approach," repeatedly making mistakes and losing money, until they hit their pain threshold and truly begin to reflect.

*   Only after reaching the extreme of pain do they understand: the repetition of mistakes must stop. This awakening is the critical turning point of growth.

## III. The Unique Nature of Trading: "Counter-Intuitive Thinking"

*   The trading market often operates contrary to everyday logic, requiring "counter-intuitive thinking."

*   For example, when the market keeps rising, human instinct is to chase the rally, but trading logic warns: the more it rises, the more you should guard against the risk of a top reversal.

*   Counter-intuitive decision-making requires long-term deliberate practice and adaptation.

## IV. The Core of Growth: Record, Identify, and Correct Error Patterns

*   The core of a trader's growth is systematically recording and reviewing their mistakes.

*   Only by clearly seeing your own "error patterns" can you avoid repeatedly falling into the same traps.

*   Developing clear self-awareness through review is the true starting point of consistent profitability.
