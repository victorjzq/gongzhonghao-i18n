# Price Action: The Illusion of Trading

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 3 years. Today is my 274th article. You should also learn Price Action.

After watching many videos in the Chinese Price Action community,

I noticed a phenomenon:

The top instructor makes it sound simple, giving a feeling of "picking up free money";

Many others explain things in a very complex way, full of "possibilities."

In many of these complex explanations, one phrase keeps coming up:

"It could go this way, or it could go that way — going long works, going short works too."

And this phrase is the very source of complexity and illusion.

Once you allow going long, going short, and waiting all at the same time within a single observation phase, three things happen in your brain:

First, your attention splits — you no longer know what you are actually looking at;

Second, the cause-and-effect sequence gets disrupted — you look at the result first, then go back to find reasons;

Third, the chart begins to explain itself — and illusion follows.

Cognitive control begins to break down.

Trading illusion means you start interpreting structure before you have determined a directional bias.

In other words, you have not yet decided whether you are looking to go long or go short, but you are already searching for structure, context, and supporting reasons.

At this point, what you see is no longer the market itself, but what you want to see.

The real mechanism for controlling illusion is sequence.

First, look at the signal bar to think about direction.

If the signal bar is bullish, only allow yourself to think about going long;

If the signal bar is bearish, only allow yourself to think about going short.

Do not allow any discussion of "what if it goes the other way."

Then begin to reference:

whether the current context, location, and pattern support this signal, forming a good swing trading opportunity. Which chart from the encyclopedia it corresponds to.

Strictly reference the structures from the encyclopedia.

This is the core mechanism for overcoming trading illusion.

Only this way can you trade what you see, rather than what you imagine.
