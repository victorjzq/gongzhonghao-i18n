# Price Action: Whoever Is Making Money Dominates the Market

The real secret of trading is not in prediction, but in reading the structure.

---

### 1. Market Order Buying: The Most Direct Attack by Bulls

The market gets aggressively pushed higher right at the open, with no pullback whatsoever.

What does this tell you?

1. Bulls don't want to wait—they're directly pushing price up with market buy orders.
2. They're afraid of missing out, actively chasing the rally.
3. Nobody is waiting for a "reasonable price" anymore.

This type of price action represents "Buy the Market" behavior.
It's not buying on a pullback—it's directly snatching orders.

You need to understand:

**Only when buying pressure is extremely strong and market expectations are highly aligned will this type of move occur.**

This kind of market usually doesn't end easily.
Most of the time, it enters a rhythm of "continuous push higher + scarce pullbacks."

---

### 2. Whoever Is Making Money Determines Direction

Al Brooks' core trading philosophy is not about prediction, but about judging structure:

- Are limit short orders making money?
- Are stop short orders making money?
- Are limit long orders making money?
- Are stop long orders making money?

In this type of strong rally, there is only one result:

Only the "chasing" market buy orders are making money.

Everyone else is left behind:

- Limit long orders: never got filled.
- Stop short orders: directly stopped out.
- Those adding shorts counter-trend: the more they short, the further they get carried away.

The secret of how the market operates is one sentence:

> **Whoever is making money dominates.**

When only those chasing the rally are profitable, the market direction is very clear—
**It's going to keep going up, with a Measured Move structure.**

---

### 3. The Core of Judging Trends: Look at Now, Not at History

The common mistake many people make is:

"Last time it pulled back here, so this time it will too."

"This rally has gone too far; it feels like it's going to drop."

But the market doesn't care about your "feelings."

**What you need to judge is not history, but the present—**

- Who currently controls the market?
- Are bulls still attacking?
- Do bears still have any resistance?

If the market is pushing higher and bears are in full retreat, you only have one choice:

**Always In Long (the trend is in the bulls' hands—only go long).**

Trading is not about predicting future ups and downs, but:

> **Acknowledging who currently holds control.**

---

### 4. Beware the "Buy Climax" Trap

The stronger the trend, the more you need to stay calm.

After three consecutive strong rally legs, one or two extra-large bull bars are often a "Buy Climax":

1. Market sentiment is maxed out; everyone is chasing the rally.
2. The third leg is typically the final emotional release.
3. Sideways movement, pullback, and choppy trading are very likely to follow.

Chasing long at this point makes it extremely easy to buy at the top.

Operational suggestions:

- If you really must chase, **use a wide stop loss**;
- The better approach: **wait for a pullback, confirm support, then enter.**

**The most dangerous time in the market is when it looks the strongest.**

---

### 5. Trading Summary: Read the Structure, Make Fewer Judgments

True experts don't rely on "predicting it will go up," but rather:

**Seeing that the market is going up, trade with it; seeing who is making money, stand on that side.**

You only need to remember:

1. Rapid market-order rallies are signals of extremely proactive bulls;
2. Whoever is making money controls the direction;
3. The stronger the trend, the more vigilant you must be—beware of reversals after climaxes;
4. All decisions are based on structure, not subjective opinions;
5. When the trend is clear, adhere to the **Always In** principle until the structure reverses.
