# Price Action: How to Trade (Part 2)

## 7. Dealing with a Bull Channel

If the market is in a bull channel:

First type: pullbacks are small and the move is strong—this can be treated as a higher time frame breakout.

Second type: the channel is broad, similar to a tilted trading range—you can also scalp short.

Ideal buying zone:

The lower third of the most recent rally.

Operations within a bull channel:

Buy directly at the close of a bull bar
Place a stop order to buy below the previous bar
Enter on a flag breakout
Even when a bear bar appears, you can try going long, because there is a high probability the bearish reversal will fail

You can also use reverse logic:

With each new high or new low in the trend, you can use conditions to trail your stop loss
Use measured moves to set profit targets

## 8. Advanced Selling Points in a Bear Trend

When a bear channel makes a new low, it indicates the trend is still continuing.

If a bull bar appears—this "looks bullish but can't break out" structure—it is the best shorting point.

You should:

Go short during strong rally bars
Go short at trend continuation points (such as double top break low, bear bar close, failed breakout)
Continuously update your stop loss using new minor highs

## 9. The Trader's Equation and the 40/60 Rule

Strong trends only account for 5% to 10% of bars on a chart
90% of bars are in channel or trading range structures
During this 90% of the time, the probability of a move up or down is 40% to 60%

As long as your trade structure is reasonable and your position sizing and risk control are in place, you can make money going long or short in most market conditions.

## 10. How to Use Default Profit Target Rules

If you don't know where to take profit, you can use the basic model:

Target is two times the initial risk, i.e., 2R

Operational example:

Take profit on half the position at 2R
Take profit on another quarter at 3R
Let the remaining position ride with a trailing stop loss
After each new high or new low, move the stop loss to the previous minor high or low

## 11. How Trends End

Strong trends eventually degrade into broad channels
Then transition into trading ranges
Only then does reversal become possible

Each phase requires a different approach
You must learn to identify the structure and adjust your strategy
Don't scalp out where you should be holding a swing trade
Don't force trades in tight ranges that aren't suitable for trading
