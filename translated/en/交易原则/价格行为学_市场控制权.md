# Price Action: Market Control

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 2 years. Today is my 211th article. You should also learn Price Action.

Market control is the core key to breakout trading. With it, you can always determine who is currently dominating the market.

There are five indicators for measuring market control: price, time, momentum, inertia, and symmetry. Momentum is further divided into speed and acceleration. Speed is how fast price rises or falls; acceleration is how fast the speed changes. Together, these compose the energy factor.

How do you determine which time is truly the last? The simplest method is to count the number of reversals directly. Both sides need to go back and forth about five times before the trend enters a trading range and may reverse. You'll find that a trend typically needs to accumulate about five reversal bars before it starts going sideways and truly reverses. However, there are exceptions—three or four pushes can turn a trend, especially in moves with small, orderly pullbacks. When bars are similar in length and the pace of progress is very regular, these types of moves typically need about five back-and-forth cycles before entering a trading range.

A strong trend usually degrades into a trading range first, and trading ranges on a higher time frame are often just flag patterns, so the probability of the original trend continuing is still greater. This counting method also works in trading ranges. After three to five legs of back-and-forth movement in a trading range, the range enters a breakout mode, which often triggers a breakout and starts a new trend.

If you want to play it safe, when trading reversals within a trading range, you typically stop after three pushes in the same direction. At that point, there have been five legs within the trading range—three in one direction and two in the other. At this point, the trading range enters breakout mode, and it is usually best to wait for the breakout to occur, then decide on your operation based on the new signals the market provides.

The final point about market control factors is symmetry. All pullback entries are essentially betting on a "failure of a failure," which is the same principle as "double negation elimination" in logic.

One method for estimating stop loss placement is to look at the chart and find the breakout bar that formed before the signal bar. Use its length as a reference, and then set the stop loss using an equal measured distance.
