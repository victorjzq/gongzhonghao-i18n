# Price Action: Make Trades That Make Sense

Everyone has winning trades — some are pure luck, making money by random chance.
But others are earned because you read the market correctly, had logic behind your decision, and managed the trade well.

The trades that truly make money are those with a mathematical edge.

For example, a trading range structure like B74 — stay away.
The direction is unclear, false breakouts are frequent — it's a stop-hunting trap, not an opportunity.

Before Trading, Ask Yourself Four Questions:
-------------

1.  Are you swing trading or scalping?

2.  Does the current structure suit your objective?

3.  Where is your stop loss? Is it appropriate?

4.  Does your take profit target have a basis?

These are all objective conditions — not complicated, but you must think them through clearly.

Manage Bad Trades
------

For example, you entered "buy the close" at B34, B35, B36,
with a stop loss below B29, but then price pulled back to your entry level.

At that point, you can add to your position above B51,
then exit around B36/B55 to break even.

---

Common Mistakes

Not wanting to take an early stop loss (below 46), yet not daring to set it far (below B29),
so you place it at an awkward level (below B42), and it gets easily stopped out.

If the market drops below 44 and then bulls step in,
you've just missed the move and lost money too.

Losing two or three points each time may not seem like much,
but accumulated over time, it's a slow bleed on your account.

To achieve consistent profitability, first eliminate those **avoidable bad trades**.

Don't fantasize about perfectly catching tops and bottoms.
What you really need to practice is:

**Make fewer mistakes, manage well, and survive.**

> Trades that make sense can win in the long run.
> Bad trades will never bring you good luck.
