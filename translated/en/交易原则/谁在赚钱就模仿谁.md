# Imitate Whoever Is Making Money

**When you discover that a certain entry method (such as stop orders or limit orders) is consistently profitable in the current market, it means the market structure is supporting that type of entry.**

---

## 1. Why "Watch Who Is Making Money"?

### 1.1 Market Structure Determines Effectiveness

- **When stop orders are making money:** If chasing momentum (stop orders) is frequently successful, it usually means the market is in a **strong trend or breakout**;

- **When limit orders are making money:** If counter-trend limit orders are frequently profitable at the upper and lower boundaries of a range, it suggests the market is in a **trading range or broad channel**.

### 1.2 Infer Structure from Results

- When determining whether the market is in a trend or a trading range, you often encounter ambiguous zones. At such times, by observing which order type is consistently profitable, you can **use results to reverse-engineer the market structure**—whether it's in a trend or a range. When a certain method's orders are consecutively profitable, it indicates that the market environment it relies on is real and effective.

---

## 2. Operational Approach: Observing Who Is Making Money

### 2.1 First Select Typical Positions for "Test" Entries

- **Chasing long near prior highs:** If breakouts succeed again and again, it indicates a stable bull market;

- **Selling with limit orders at highs:** If every push toward the high is rejected, it suggests the market favors range-bound trading, making sell-high-buy-low more effective.

### 2.2 Continuously Track Profit and Loss Results

- **Consecutive profits:** If a certain method is profitable multiple times in a row, you can conclude that the current market structure supports it;

- **Sudden failure:** When stop order momentum chasing or limit order counter-trend entries suddenly get stopped out repeatedly, it indicates the market structure has changed, and you need to adjust your approach promptly.

---

## 3. Which Order Type to Use?

### 3.1 Stop Order Entry

- Suitable for **strong trend or breakout** markets. When you observe continuous one-directional breakouts and momentum-chasing stop orders keep profiting, it means the momentum strategy is effective.

- For example, if the market repeatedly breaks above prior highs and continues rallying at new highs, with-trend stop buy entries typically show a high win rate.

### 3.2 Limit Order Entry

- Suitable for **trading range, broad channel, or frequent false breakout** environments.

- When price repeatedly fails to break above resistance or below support, placing limit orders for counter-trend entries yields steady profits, indicating that a range-bound structure is dominant during this period.

### 3.3 One Side Completely Unprofitable & the Other Highly Profitable

- If you find that **neither stop orders nor limit orders can profit**, it often means an extreme one-directional market (Always In) was already established earlier. You're either already in the trend, or you simply don't trade.

- If only **stop orders are profiting wildly** while limit orders keep failing, it indicates a clear **strong trend**; conversely, if only **limit orders are consistently successful**, the market leans more toward a trading range.

---

## 4. Key Points for "Observe Results First, Then Decide to Follow"

### 4.1 Start with Small Positions to Test the Direction

- If you judge the current market as trending but aren't sure, try small-position stop order momentum trades first; if profitable multiple times, gradually increase position size.

### 4.2 Once You Determine "Who Is Making Money," Switch Decisively

- If you were previously profiting with limit orders in a range, and several successful breakouts suddenly occur, you must quickly switch to momentum-chasing stop orders;

- Market conditions change fast; your trading approach must follow suit promptly.

### 4.3 Keep Tracking—"Who Is Making Money" Will Change

- Markets typically cycle through "trend -> trading range -> trend again." The order type that was previously profitable may lose its edge as the market evolves, so you must stay alert at all times.

---

## Summary: Use Market Feedback to Validate Structure

> **"The prerequisite for adopting a certain order type is first seeing that it's already making money."** The idea behind this statement is:
>
> 1. **Market structure determines whether an order type is effective**;
>
> 2. **Whoever is making money means the current environment is helping them profit**;
>
> 3. **Since it's working, follow and imitate first.**

When you skillfully apply this "results validation" approach, you can more quickly identify whether the market is trending or range-bound, and accordingly choose stop orders for momentum or limit orders for counter-trend trading. You can also rapidly detect when a previously effective method stops working due to a sudden structural shift, allowing you to adjust your strategy in time. As a result, both your win rate and risk/reward ratio can improve.
