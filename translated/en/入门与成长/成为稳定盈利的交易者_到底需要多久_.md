# How Long Does It Really Take to Become a Consistently Profitable Trader?

It took me 5 years.

Trading is not a path I recommend taking lightly.

Trading burns through not just money, but also your confidence, your emotions, and even your sense of purpose in life.

There is very little truly usable trading knowledge available domestically. I paid a lot of tuition and stepped into many traps:

Narrative logic sounds perfect, but execution in practice is terrifying;

Indicator signals always lag — they're just after-the-fact explanations;

Candlestick patterns divorced from specific structure are nothing but self-hypnosis;

Trendlines only tell you when to enter, but cannot tell you how to close the loop and exit;

Trading without win rate and risk/reward data to back it up is ultimately just gut-feeling decision-making.

Those systems that sound profound are full of pitfalls in practice:

Wyckoff Theory — you only understand it clearly after the move is over;

Fibonacci — everything looks logical, yet every trade goes wrong;

Gann Theory — strong explanatory power but zero feel in live trading;

Harmonic patterns — ratio requirements are strict, and actual execution is impossible;

Elliott Wave Theory — by the end of the wave count, you can't even justify it to yourself.

The more you learn, the more confused you get; the deeper you go, the more astray you become.

Back to the question itself: how long does it take to become consistently profitable?

Some take half a year, some a year, some three to five years, and some can never get started.

My good friend Peng Ge took 2 years.

It has nothing to do with IQ or education.

The only thing that matters is: can you let go of subjectivity and completely trust the chart?

When the market drops, you go short — regardless of what the news says;

When the market rises, you go long — regardless of how you feel inside;

What looks like it's about to crash is actually just a trading range, and it will go up;

What seems about to fly is actually a bad bounce, and it will eventually drop.

What you trade is never the news, nor emotions, but price itself.

The real turning point is called "Price Action."

It wasn't until I encountered Al Brooks' Price Action trading method that I finally abandoned all those things I "half understood."

Price Action trading doesn't predict, doesn't rely on faith — it focuses on only one thing:

Long-term positive mathematical expectation.

Look at A-share momentum traders' leading stock strategies, or Bit Langlang's 100x trading method in crypto — they are all essentially Price Action.

Real traders rely on judgment:

Judging whether you are executing a system, or trying to prove yourself right.

When evaluating trading teachers, focus on just one thing:

If everything they say is aimed at proving they're right, they most likely haven't made money yet.

For example, certain teachers on YouTube constantly obsess over new methods, constantly negating their past selves.

This is the bottleneck — unable to break through, very hard to actually make money.

They also teach Price Action, but can't explain it clearly, lost in knowledge itself rather than in knowledge that actually works.

I'm not putting them down — when I was starting out, I also watched their videos and recommended them to many people.

I'm simply discussing trading psychology objectively.

Change your cognitive system first.

For new traders, the most important thing is not "being right" but "staying alive."

Do only two things:

First, keep each individual stop loss within 1%;

Second, set the risk/reward ratio at 1:2.

Even if you trade randomly, after 100 trades, you might still be profitable.

Get these two aspects right first, then study entry selection and timing — only then does it become meaningful.

Otherwise, you'll die on the road before you even start.

One final reminder:

Self-blame is the most painful part of trading.

Losing money itself isn't scary — what's scary is that self-blame will gradually crush you.

Losing over a million in a single day — only a real trader knows what that feels like.

No one will truly understand you, including your family.

The market won't give you any excuses.

So I don't recommend you walk this path, unless you're truly prepared.

But if you really want to go deeper, I recommend you at least learn about "Price Action."

It may not make you money right away, but it can save you hundreds of thousands in losses.

And the money you didn't lose is the most real source of happiness in your life.

May you pay less tuition, feel less self-blame, and gain more clarity.
