# Price Action: How to Practice

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 2 years. Today is my 184th article, and you should also start learning Price Action.

When observing charts and preparing to draw lines, you typically start by drawing lines to identify channels, wedges, double tops, double bottoms, and triangles, then highlight these structures with lines. Next, look for special bars, such as very large bars, very small bars, inside bars where the low is higher than the previous bar and the high is lower than the previous bar, consecutive inside bars, outside bars, a large bar followed by an even larger bar, or "inside-outside-inside" combination bar structures. Then, draw red and green boxes on the chart to mark what you consider ideal entry points.

Traders must practice. Just as professional football players don't spend the entire week sitting at home watching TV after a Sunday game, but train every day; a professional musician, even if performing only a few times a month, practices daily. Likewise, traders cannot simply show up when the market opens and expect to perform well. You should practice after the market closes, spending time reviewing the price action on the charts you traded that day, looking for patterns, reflecting on what you did, and what else you could have done.

As a trader, the core of practice is studying charts and identifying patterns. Clearly, the better you understand what the market is doing and the better you can predict what the market will do, the more likely you are to be profitable. The faster you recognize patterns, the faster you can build a trading plan.

Look for channels and wedges. A wedge is essentially a channel formed by three or more points. When a wedge is ascending, you would expect a downward reversal, so you only focus on the upper trendline.

If you keep practicing like this for several months, you will begin to recognize these patterns forming in real time during the trading session. This will allow you to anticipate trading opportunities in advance, so when the trading signal triggers, you can enter and manage the trade effectively. And this is the true meaning of "practicing trading."
