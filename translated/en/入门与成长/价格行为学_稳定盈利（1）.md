# Price Action: Consistent Profitability (1)

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 3 years. Today is my 280th article, and you should also start learning Price Action.

## Consistent Profitability Requires Systematically Giving Up Certain Trading Opportunities

**First Reversal Attempt in a Strong Trend or Narrow Channel**

80% of first reversal attempts fail.

Give up the "V-shaped reversal" fantasy. At least wait for a second signal, such as a double bottom, double top, H2, or L2.

**The Middle of a Trading Range**

The middle zone is the "fair price," with 50/50 probability in either direction and very poor risk/reward ratio.

Only take action in the upper 1/3 or lower 1/3 of the range.

**Breakouts Without Follow-Through**

If no same-direction trend bar confirms after the breakout bar, classify it as a failed breakout and pass.

**Predictive Trades Based on News or Data**

News is irrelevant — price action already contains all information.

Actively exit before major data releases and wait for direction to become clear.

**Poor Quality Signal Bars**

Even if the location is correct, if the signal bar has too small a body, long tails, or closes in the opposite direction, pass on the entry.

**Chasing "Surprise Bars" Late in a Trend**

A very large bull bar after a trend has lasted 20+ bars is often a "buying climax."

This is a "gift bar" from bulls taking profits, handed to the bears.

**Tight Trading Range (TTR)**

When bars overlap heavily with no direction, stay flat and wait for a breakout.

**Intraday Junk Time (After Bar 72)**

Institutional position-closing in the final 30 minutes creates erratic price action. Do not participate.

**Attitude Toward "Not Trading"**

Wait for prey like a snake or sniper, rather than firing frequently.

The market is like a buffet — you can't eat every dish. For price action you don't understand, the attitude should be: "That's okay, don't think about it."

Making money is very simple; the truly difficult part is how not to lose money.

Accept missing trades calmly — the market opens every day.

Perfection is accidental; disappointment is permanent. Most price movement lacks logical support.
