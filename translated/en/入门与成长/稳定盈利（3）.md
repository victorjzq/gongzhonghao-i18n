## How Consistently Profitable Traders View Wins and Losses on Individual Trades

**Wins and Losses Are Random Costs**

Losses are like rent and utilities for running a restaurant — they are an inevitable cost of doing business.

The key is not whether losses exist, but whether they are kept within a controllable range.

**There Is No 100% Certainty**

For 80%–90% of the time, the market is not black and white.

No matter how skilled you are, you must assume there is a 40% chance of losing — this is the 40-60 rule.

**Respect for the Counterparty**

The counterparty is usually a smarter, better-resourced institution.

Once you judge incorrectly, you must decisively admit the mistake and exit — never hold stubbornly.

**Whether you make money is not important. What matters is whether you did the right thing.**

**Structure and Context Review**

Rather than the signal bar itself, focus more on whether the context was judged correctly.

**Probability Accumulation**

The goal is not one or two trades, but a steadily rising equity curve after 500–1,000 trades.

Oppose the mindset of rushing to make money on a trade each day. "Fear of missing out" is one of the primary causes of losses.

Oppose the "break even" mentality — the market never cares about your cost basis.

Use a "no-feeling position size" to ensure decisions are based on chart logic, not on the fluctuations of your account balance.

**Mathematical Expectation Thinking**

A 40% win rate, combined with a 2:1 risk/reward ratio, is sufficient to achieve long-term growth.

Before placing the order, the expected value has already been calculated.

Don't obsess over "can I win this one" — only care about "does this action conform to a high-probability model."
