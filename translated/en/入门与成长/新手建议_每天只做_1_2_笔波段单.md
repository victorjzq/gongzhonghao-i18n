# Advice for Beginners: Only Take 1–2 Swing Trades Per Day

1. **Limit Trade Count, Stabilize Your Rhythm**
   Take only 1–2 swing trades per day with clear structure and sufficient potential range. Avoid emotional and overtrading.
   Too many orders and too fast a pace will prevent you from keeping up with the market rhythm, making it harder to stay rational.

2. **Why Swing Trading Is Suitable for Beginners**
   - The pace is relatively relaxed — you don't need to watch every bar, leaving ample time for analysis and execution
   - It relies on patterns, support/resistance, and bar combinations — structures that are clear and easy to identify
   - The workflow is fixed: find an entry signal -> set stop loss -> define target price (at least 1:2)
   - After completing the trade, you can temporarily step away, maintaining a calmer mindset and avoiding overtrading

3. **How to Select "That One Trade"**
   When taking only one trade per day, it must simultaneously meet:
   a) A reasonable entry signal: second entry (H2/L2), breakout pullback, trendline support, two-bar bull combination, etc.
   b) A clear larger time frame context: with-trend, larger time frame support, end of channel, first-hour momentum bar after the open
   c) Sufficient room: the potential to achieve >= 2R profit after entry

4. **Stop Loss and Target Setting**
   - Don't set the stop too tight — normal volatility will sweep it out
   - For long trades, place the stop below the prior low; for short trades, place it above the prior high
   - The target should be at least 2x the risk (2R) — reference prior highs/lows, measured move targets, or the height of the previous swing
   - After placing the order, don't adjust frequently — observe whether the price action develops as expected

5. **Accept Uncertainty in Trading**
   - Sometimes you can only capture a small profit and need to exit promptly
   - Sometimes there will be small losses — this is normal cost of doing business
   - Trades that fully reach 2R or higher profit occur roughly only 1–2 times per week
   Swing trading relies on fewer entries and capturing big opportunities — don't expect every trade to be a windfall

6. **Practice Focus Before Advancing**
   At this stage, there's no need to split orders, scale in/out in batches, or take early profits to re-enter at lower levels.
   The most important things right now are:
   - Practice fundamentals, improve your chart reading feel
   - Practice consistent stop loss execution
   - Practice discipline — strictly follow your plan when placing orders

7. **Best Entry Timing**
   Typically in the first 1–2 hours after the open, the market is most likely to present clear structure.
   If there are no clear opportunities during this window, pause operations — missing a trade is always better than forcing a wrong one.

## Conclusion

Only taking 1–2 swing trades per day isn't about being lazy — it's about helping you focus on the best opportunities.
Build patience through consistency, sharpen your rhythm through discipline. Complete this stage of practice thoroughly, and you'll be able to split orders and manage positions much more flexibly in the future.
