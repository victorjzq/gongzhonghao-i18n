# Price Action: Direction for Beginners

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 2 years. Today is my 249th article, and you should also start learning Price Action.

All complex moves are essentially variations of channel patterns, often manifesting as pullback patterns. When the market begins to pull back, it enters a trading range.

The most complex price move may appear as a simple move on the next higher time frame. In other words, no matter how dramatic the pullback appears on the current chart, on the larger time frame it only shows a minor pullback, and the market will eventually resume its original trend. This is like repeated oscillation, displaying the same pattern across three different time frames, but ultimately all these patterns will play out. The real art lies in judging which time frame will trigger first, and patiently waiting for the move to complete. A simpler approach is to simply assume that all narrow channels are manifestations of a larger time frame breakout.

Through larger time frame channel breakout patterns, no matter how deep or dramatic the pullback, as long as support holds, the move will not completely reverse. This should be the go-to strategy for beginners. For beginners, the focus should be on studying breakout follow-through patterns.

Intraday trading is about continuously testing support and resistance levels, ultimately forming the daily bar. During this process, narrow channel patterns frequently appear. Most intraday price action takes the form of narrow channels. This is the market's process of probing support and resistance, during which various technical patterns form. However, these patterns are essentially narrow channels, and they are far more common compared to other time frames. Narrow channel patterns are everywhere in intraday price action.
