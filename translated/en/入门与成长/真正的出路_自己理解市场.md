# The Real Way Out: Understanding the Market Yourself

AL: After ten years, I finally made up my mind to abandon my trust in so-called "mentors." I realized that most people in this industry are frauds. All the advertisements, books, and conference promotions are worthless. No matter how fancy the charts, how polished the interfaces, or how loudly the theories are proclaimed, none of it has anything to do with making money.

So I threw all of it away and **focused every day on printing naked bar charts and analyzing price itself**. For example:

- If today closed with a bear bar, why?

- If it was a bull bar, why?

- Three consecutive bull bars — why?

I began to understand the market's intent, one piece at a time.

## Focus on How You Lose Money, and How You Make Money

This actually became clear quite quickly. I started discovering that **the reasons I lost money were very predictable**, such as:

- Buying at the top of a trading range;

- Going short at the bottom of a range;

- Trying to pick a top after a strong bull breakout;

- Going short in an obviously bullish range;

- Thinking the market would "revert to the mean," only to get crushed by a breakout.

These losses all followed patterns. Conversely, **if the price action looks strong, you can only trade with the trend** — go long in a strong rally, go short in a strong decline.

The longer you do this, the more the daily printed charts will reveal which locations have **high probability**. You'll discover:

> "Oh, at this location, if I buy and place my stop here, there's a high probability of capturing a measured move."

You determine that at a certain moment the probability has reached 60%. Then from your entry to your stop loss risk, and the potential target, the distances are roughly equal. This is a "reasonable odds" trade.

## The Market Gives You Signals Every Day

**Almost every day, the chart will display a type of price action that makes you believe "the market will most likely do this next."** Your job is not to predict the future, but to recognize these signals. Once you identify a setup with over 60% probability, set your stop loss and target reasonably, and you can achieve consistent, stable profitability.

## Key Takeaways:

1. **Discard illusions, return to fundamentals**: Fancy indicators and authoritative packaging are all mirages. Only price itself reveals the market's true intent.

2. **Face your losses, discover the patterns**: The places where you repeatedly make mistakes are the source of learning. Trading logic hides within the patterns of how you keep losing money.

3. **Identify signals, establish odds**: Don't predict the market. Instead, identify high-probability price action, construct reasonable odds, and execute repeatedly.
