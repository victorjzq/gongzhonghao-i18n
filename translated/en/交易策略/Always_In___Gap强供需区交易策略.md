**The key to trend trading lies in identifying whether the market is in a strong state (Always In) and fully utilizing unfilled gaps for trading.**

## 1. How to Determine Always In?

### (1) Two Large Bars Confirm the Trend

*   Observe two consecutive bars with a clear directional trend;
*   If the second bar has a gap (Gap Bar) or a strong close, it indicates the market has entered a clear "Always In" trend state.

### (2) Trade with the Trend + Pullback Entry

*   In an "Always In" state, when price pulls back to short-term support or a key moving average (such as the 5-period or 20-period moving average), it presents an entry opportunity.
*   Once the trend is confirmed, trading with the trend makes it easier to gain an edge.

### Warning: Bear Flag or Pullback Confirmation

*   When the market is in an "Always In Short" (strong bearish) state, every bear flag bounce or minor pullback is a potential short opportunity.

## 2. Unfilled Gap: Strong Supply and Demand Zone

### (1) The Meaning of a Gap and Market Strength Assessment

*   A gap that remains unfilled for an extended period indicates a severe supply and demand imbalance in the market;
*   Such gaps typically form strong support or resistance zones.

### (2) Practical Examples: Long and Short Strategies

*   **Long Strategy (Gap as Strong Support)**:
    *   When price gaps up and the pullback fails to fill the gap, it indicates strong buying pressure;
    *   At this point, look for Pin Bars (long tails/wicks) or High 1 (H1) entry signals near the gap, and go long with the trend.

*   **Short Strategy (Gap as Strong Resistance)**:
    *   When price gaps down and the subsequent bounce fails to close the gap, it indicates heavy selling pressure;
    *   When price bounces back to the gap area and encounters resistance, look for clear bearish signals (Low 1, strong bear bars) to enter.

* * *

**Practical Insights and Summary**

*   In an "Always In" state, maintain a trend-following mindset and seize entry opportunities through pullback confirmations;
*   An unfilled gap marks a strong supply and demand zone in the market, offering significant trading value.
*   Clearly identify the nature of the gap and the strength of the trend to precisely capture high-probability trading setups within market fluctuations.
