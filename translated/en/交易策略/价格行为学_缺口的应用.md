**Understanding and Trading "Micro Gaps" Correctly**

**1. Definition of a Micro Gap**
A micro gap occurs when the current bar's open is above or below the previous bar's close. Even if the size is small, a gap represents a strong directional attempt by the market.

**2. Assessing Gap Continuation**
To determine whether a gap has continuation potential, observe the following:
*   Is the current bar's low above the lows of all bars to the left?
*   Is it above the previous bar's high?
    If the answer to both is yes, bulls continue buying at higher prices after the breakout, support holds, and the trend is likely to continue.

**3. True Gaps and the Staircase Pattern**
When trend bars have no overlap with each other, a true gap forms; the more such gaps occur, the stronger the trend's continuity.
If gaps are quickly filled and price returns to its original level after each small breakout, the market enters a staircase pattern, indicating insufficient momentum and a likely evolution into a trading range.

**4. Bounce Direction After a Sharp Selloff**
After a strong bounce following a sharp selloff in a tight bear channel, the market is more likely to transition into a broad bear channel or a trading range rather than directly reversing into a bull trend. Bears often short at the midpoint of the prior bear leg, viewing the bounce as a minor pullback.

**5. Signs of Bear Exhaustion**
If all gaps are filled, bulls will find that they can profit even when buying at lower prices. This indicates the bear trend is weakening, and the market may shift into a trading range or even reverse upward.

**6. Small Pullback Bull Trend**
If a bull trend features continuous upward gaps with only small pullbacks each time, even if the rally is not dramatic, it may form a small pullback bull trend. This type of trend often lasts longer and is difficult to break.

**7. Exhaustion and Measuring Gaps**
After a trend has been running for twenty or more bars, if a huge bar appears followed immediately by a large opposite bar, it is most likely an exhaustion gap, and the move may be reaching a top. If the bars following the huge bar are still bull body bars, it is more likely a measuring gap, and the trend often continues for an equal distance.

**8. Trap Gap Signals**
These occur in trading ranges or broad channels, appear late in a trend, show weak follow-through after the largest bar, and are quickly followed by a large opposite bar or a bar with a long tail. They often signal that momentum in the original direction is exhausted.

**9. Strong Gaps vs. Weak Gaps**
*   **Strong gap**: No overlap, consecutive higher gaps, tight channel; suitable for holding positions in the trend direction.
*   **Weak gap**: Quickly filled, long tails with small bodies, tug-of-war between bulls and bears; watch out for a staircase-style decline.

**10. Bull Opportunities in the First Bear Signal**
In a small pullback bull trend, the first break below short-term support or the first touch of the moving average — these initial bear signals are often low-risk adding points for bulls.

**11. Practical Key Points**
*   When you see consecutive unfilled micro gaps, you can enter a small position in the trend direction with a stop loss placed just beyond the lower edge of the most recent gap.
*   If gaps keep getting filled and price returns to its original level each time, pause trading and wait to make decisions at the upper or lower boundaries of the range.
*   After a sharp selloff, first watch for shorting opportunities at the midpoint of the bear leg rather than rushing to go long.
*   When the market forms a small pullback bull trend, maintain a bull bias until the first deep pullback occurs.
