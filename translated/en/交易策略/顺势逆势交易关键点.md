# Key Points of With-Trend and Counter-Trend Trading

The core of with-trend trading is entering on the second reversal failure (H2/L2), because in a trend, the failure of the second pullback often means the counter-trend force has been exhausted, giving the with-trend direction an advantage. For counter-trend trading, we need to consider higher safety requirements, typically waiting for three or even four reversal failures before entering. In Al Brooks' framework, this usually manifests in the following scenarios:

## Best Entry Points for Counter-Trend Trading: Waiting for Three or Four Failures

**Core Logic:**

- Trends have inertia; 80% of reversal attempts fail.
- Counter-trend trading has a lower win rate, so you must ensure the market reversal signal is strong enough.
- The best counter-trend entries typically come after the third or fourth reversal failure.

## 1. Counter-Trend Trading in a Trading Range (TR)

- Third or fourth breakout failure at the top of the range (Low 3 / High 4)
- Third or fourth test failure at the bottom of the range (High 3 / Low 4)
- **Execution:**
  - Enter after the third or fourth failed reversal bar.
  - Place the stop loss beyond the most recent high/low.
  - Target: the middle of the range or the opposite boundary.

✅ **Example:**
- Price forms three failed breakout attempts at the top of the trading range (High 3), each quickly pulled back.
- After the third failure, a strong bear bar appears, confirming the entry signal.
- **Execution strategy:** Sell below the signal bar's close, place the stop loss 2–3 ticks above the prior high, target the bottom of the range.

## 2. Major Trend Reversal (MTR): Waiting for Three or Four Pushes

- Trend reversals typically require three to four failed attempts, usually manifesting as:
  - Wedge (three pushes)
  - Broadening Triangle (four pushes)
  - Pullback test failure after a trend line breakout
- **Execution:**
  - Enter after the third or fourth failed reversal bar appears.
  - Avoid entering on the first or second failure, as the trend may still continue.
  - Place the stop loss beyond the high/low of the last failed reversal.

✅ **Example:**
- Price gradually moves higher in an uptrend, but forms three higher pushes (Wedge Top), with increased volume on the third push.
- After the third push fails, a strong bear bar appears, indicating the market is entering a reversal state.
- **Execution strategy:**
  - Short 1–2 ticks below the signal bar's low after the third push fails.
  - Place the stop loss above the third push high.
  - Set the first target as the measured move (MM), and the second target as the prior low.

## 3. Safety Strategy for Counter-Trend Trading

How to assess the safety of counter-trend trading when the trend is strong?

- Counter-trend trading is harder than with-trend trading; waiting for three or four failures before entering typically yields a higher win rate.
- The stronger the trend, the more failures you need before it's safe:
  - **Strong trend:** Wait for 4 failures (Low 4 / High 4).
  - **Normal trend:** Wait for 3 failures (Low 3 / High 3).
  - **Trading range:** Wait for 2 failures (Low 2 / High 2).
- In a trend, do not attempt counter-trend trades unless there is a clear signal.

✅ **Example:**
- In a strong bear trend, the market fails on the 3rd or 4th attempt to break below the prior low, forming a Wedge Bottom.
- A strong bull bar appears, with the close near its high and a long lower tail, indicating bear exhaustion.
- **Execution strategy:**
  - Enter 1 tick above the signal bar's high.
  - Place the stop loss below the most recent low.
  - Set the target as the prior Higher High or the measured move target.

## 4. Counter-Trend vs. With-Trend Trading: Number of Entry Failures

| Trade Type | Entry Timing | Number of Failures Before Entry | Win Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| With-trend trading | H2 / L2 | 2 failures | High |
| Counter-trend trading (trading range) | Low 3 / High 3 | 3 failures | Medium |
| Counter-trend trading (trend reversal) | Wedge Bottom / Top | 3–4 failures | Low |

## Summary

- **With-trend trading:** Wait for two reversal failures (H2/L2), because trend inertia is strong and the win rate is high.
- **Counter-trend trading:** Wait for at least three or four failures (Low 3 / High 4 / Wedge) to reduce the risk of false reversals.
- The stronger the trend, the more failures you need to wait for; typically 4 failures is the safest counter-trend entry.

## ✅ Trading Strategy Optimization

- In a trading range, wait for three failures (Low 3 / High 3) and trade in the opposite direction.
- For trend reversals, wait for a three-push wedge (Wedge) or a four-push Broadening Wedge before entering.
- When the trend is strong, avoid counter-trend trading, or at least wait for four failures (Low 4 / High 4).
