# Price Action Trading Decision System

"How do you make decisions in a fast-paced world?"

Bridgewater founder Ray Dalio answered it this way: "I found that the only way I could do this was to write down the criteria, conditions, and principles I used to make decisions, and encode them into computer programs, thereby building a 'decision machine' that could operate in parallel with me and make judgments alongside me. It's a bit like a chess grandmaster developing a computer chess system that plays in sync with himself; in this process, both the grandmaster and the automated decision-making process continuously iterate and reinforce each other, ultimately becoming stronger together."

In his book *Principles*, Dalio also wrote: "Before long, computers with powerful processing capabilities became far more efficient than I was. This was great, because it was like having a chess grandmaster helping me play, except that the set of criteria this grandmaster relied on were ones I understood and believed to be logical, so there was no reason for us to fundamentally disagree at any point."
