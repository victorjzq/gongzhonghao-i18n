**Many traders tend to focus on achieving a high risk-reward ratio on individual trades, hoping to earn substantial returns through long-term positions. However, the cost of this approach is that capital remains tied up for extended periods, profit cycles lengthen, and psychological pressure increases. In contrast, a "quick in, quick out" strategy that emphasizes trading turnover rate can effectively overcome these problems.**

**1. Efficient Use of Capital Is the Foundation of Trading Success**

Trading is essentially a process of capital circulation and reuse. If a single trade only earns a 1:1 risk-reward ratio, how can overall returns continue to grow? The answer lies in improving the trading turnover rate. Rapid capital turnover means funds can continuously participate in new trades, creating more profit opportunities and accumulating greater profits.

**2. High Turnover Rate Requires the Ability to Capture Short-Term Volatility**

Improving the turnover rate means that after each entry, the market needs to quickly reach the breakeven point. Therefore, traders must possess the ability to identify and capture large short-term moves, including:

*   Precisely identifying critical points where market breakouts or trends begin;
*   Monitoring short-term news and capital activity to position in advance.

By selecting market structures where a 1:1 target range is easily achievable in a short time, you increase the probability of trading success, thereby ensuring efficient capital turnover.

**3. High Turnover Strategy Strengthens Psychology and Capital Management**

Under a high turnover strategy, once the predetermined target (such as 1:1) is reached, you exit decisively. Even if the market continues to move in your favor, you must rationally accept the fact of "giving up excess profits." Because the strategy itself is positioned around accumulating frequent small wins, this psychological adjustment helps to:

*   Reduce emotional fluctuations and avoid the negative impact of "regret" emotions;
*   Execute discipline more strictly, improving trading stability and execution.

**4. Continuous Small Wins Accumulate to Achieve Long-Term Steady Growth**

Although the risk-reward ratio per trade is only 1:1, as long as the win rate remains at a relatively high level combined with rapid capital turnover, overall profits will also be quite substantial. The high turnover strategy significantly shortens the capital recovery cycle, improves capital utilization efficiency, achieves continuous accumulation of small profits, and ultimately reaches stable, long-term return growth.

**Conclusion**

The trading turnover rate is a critical link connecting capital efficiency, profitability, and trading psychology. A high turnover rate strategy not only improves capital utilization efficiency but also helps maintain a stable trading mindset and discipline, ultimately achieving sustained and steady profits. This strategy is suitable for traders who are skilled at capturing short-term market moves and who prioritize stability — it is an effective trading method worth exploring and practicing.
