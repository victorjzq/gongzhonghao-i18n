# Price Action: How to Understand With-Trend and Counter-Trend Trading

With-trend trading is the core strategy for most professional traders, because trades in the direction of the trend tend to have a higher win rate and a better risk-reward ratio. Counter-trend trading can also provide high-quality opportunities in specific situations, especially when a trend is exhausting or at extreme levels.

## Advantages of With-Trend Trading

- The context supports momentum continuation, making the market direction clearer
- Even if the entry point is slightly off, there is a higher probability that the trend will correct it
- Less psychological pressure, because you are trading in the same direction as most market participants

## Risks of With-Trend Trading

- The trend can exhaust at any time, especially after it has extended too far
- It is easy to get lured into chasing entries at highs or lows, resulting in significant pullbacks

## Advantages of Counter-Trend Trading

- At extreme price levels, the stop loss distance is often smaller
- Once a reversal succeeds, the reward can be very substantial
- Suitable for capturing turning points at the end of a trend

## Risks of Counter-Trend Trading

- You are fighting against the current momentum, so the failure rate is higher
- If the context assessment is wrong, you can get stopped out very quickly
- Requires extremely high execution speed and discipline

## How to Weigh the Two

Professional traders typically first confirm the background trend, then make their choice based on the following logic:

- If the trend is in its early or middle stage, prioritize with-trend trading
- If the trend is in its late stage and shows clear exhaustion signals, you can attempt counter-trend trading
- In a trading range, fading extreme levels usually has a higher success rate than chasing breakouts

## Application with Entry Strategies

For with-trend trading, you can enter at the signal bar after the first pullback; for counter-trend trading, you tend to wait for confirmation from a failed breakout or a double failure.

For example, at the end of a bull trend, if two upward pushes fail near the high, the reversal breakout on the second failure is a high-quality counter-trend opportunity.

## Adjusting Position Size and Stop Loss

For with-trend trading, position size can be relatively larger, with the stop loss placed outside the structure to give price some room to fluctuate; for counter-trend trading, position size should be smaller and the stop loss should be tight, because losses can accumulate very quickly when the trade fails.

## Conclusion

With-trend and counter-trend are not absolute opposing choices, but rather two approaches that should be flexibly switched based on the context. The key is that you must first understand what level you are at, what stage the market is in, and what kind of market rhythm you are facing.
