In trading, going with the trend is one of the most fundamental and effective principles. However, most traders often fail due to trend "traps" and "misreadings."
Today, we outline **four key points** to help you clarify the rhythm and operational logic of with-trend trading.

* * *

01 | The First Leg of a Trend: You Must Follow It
-------------

*   When the market produces a **strong breakout**, especially when the first leg of a move begins, **do not trade counter-trend**.
*   There is a high probability of a **second leg up/down** (2nd leg up/down).
*   You should **hold your position firmly**, maintain an "Always In" status, and even consider scaling in.

> The trend starts → you must follow → focus on holding positions.

* * *

02 | Beware of the Second Leg Trap
----------

*   The second leg within a trading range often appears particularly strong, looking like it will break out, but it easily fails.
*   This is a classic **second leg trap (2nd leg trap)**.

> The trend reaches the second leg → watch out for false breakouts → avoid blindly chasing entries.

* * *

03 | The Third Leg of a Trend: High-Frequency Zone for Reversal Signals
-----------------

*   When a trend develops into the **third leg**, start watching for reversals.
*   Trading strategy:
*   **With-trend side**: take profit in batches, reduce position size.
*   **Counter-trend side**: closely monitor strong reversal signals, prepare to reverse your position.

> End of the trend → take profit in batches → look for reversals.

* * *

04 | Wedge Patterns Don't Always Produce Big Moves
-------------

*   Not all **wedge patterns (Wedge)** will trigger a major reversal.
*   Many wedges only produce choppy action or short-term bounces, after which the original trend continues or the market enters a trading range.
*   Strategy suggestions:
*   Use a **scalp + swing** hybrid approach.
*   Trade with small positions for quick profits, and wait for the market to truly provide a clear direction.

> Facing a wedge → switch flexibly → mainly probe with light positions.

* * *

Summary of Trading Rhythm
------

1.  **First leg: follow the trend**
2.  **Second leg: watch out for traps**
3.  **Third leg: guard against reversals**
4.  **Wedge patterns: focus on short-term trades**
