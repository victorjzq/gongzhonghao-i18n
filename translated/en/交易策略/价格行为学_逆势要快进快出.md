# Price Action: Counter-Trend Trades Should Be Quick In and Quick Out

To form a standard gap fill, the typical process is: first a pullback, then a trend resumption, and finally a test—only then is the gap considered filled. As long as no price falls back below the breakout line after the breakout, the gap remains open; it is just that the gap size has become smaller.

Significant overlap among bars indicates more "two-sided trading" in the market, which will limit the upside potential.

An outside bar appearing late in a trend is usually a signal that a trading range is about to form, while an inside bar and its follow-through move typically fail, and price often pulls back to test the level of the inside bar.

When you are in a counter-trend trade and have an unrealized profit, exit when price reaches the moving average—sometimes even earlier. Do not expect to make huge profits from counter-trend trades; just making the trade work is enough.

Counter-trend trading by its very nature should be quick in and quick out, because if you are betting that the current price will be the highest point in the next three years, the probability of that is extremely low. If you always trade this way, especially near all-time highs, you will lose money most of the time. A simpler and higher-probability logic is: "The pullback is about to end, and the market will make a new all-time high, because the current environment is a bull trend."
