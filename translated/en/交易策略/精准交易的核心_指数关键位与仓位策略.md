Trading is never simply a game of pursuing a high win rate. The true core of profitability lies in precisely identifying clear market opportunities, controlling risk exposure, and decisively reducing position size during uncertain market conditions.

This article provides a professional and clear trading framework from three dimensions: the contest at key index levels, expectation confirmation, and position size management, helping you navigate the market with stability.

## 1. Key Index Levels: The Real Battleground of the Market

Key index levels are zones where bullish and bearish forces clearly clash. Accurately identifying and capitalizing on these levels can significantly improve your risk-reward ratio.

*   **Definition and Identification of Key Levels**:
*   Highs and lows that the market has repeatedly tested in the past;
*   Important price levels near the 20-period moving average (MA20);
*   Obvious contest zones formed by technical patterns such as double tops, double bottoms, and three-push patterns.

*   **Precise Interpretation of Signal Bars**:
*   When a bar with a clear reversal or failed contest implication appears near a key level — such as a bar with a long tail or a doji — it indicates the market is about to make a significant directional decision.

*   **Strict Standards for Expectation Confirmation**:
*   After a signal bar appears at a key level, if the next bar breaks above/below the high/low of the previous bar (triggered by one tick), the expectation is confirmed and the entry signal is valid.
*   If no breakout occurs, the expectation is unconfirmed — stay on the sidelines or probe with only a minimal position size.

## 2. The Index Determines the Overall Market's Profit-Loss Structure

The trend of the index determines the overall market's profit-loss probability and rhythm:

*   When the index expectation is confirmed, market conditions are clear and the risk-reward ratio improves substantially — you should actively participate or moderately increase your position size;
*   When the index expectation is unconfirmed, market conditions are ambiguous — participating in any individual stock or other instrument at this point tends to cause unnecessary losses. You should reduce your position size or pause trading promptly.

## 3. Position Size Management: The Key to a Stable Equity Curve

Precise and effective position size management is the decisive factor in improving equity curve performance. The core principle is to adjust position size based on how clear the market conditions are:

*   **When conditions are clear (index expectation confirmed)**: Maintain normal position size or moderately increase it to capture clear profit opportunities;
*   **When conditions are unclear (index expectation unconfirmed)**: Reduce position size to 1/4 or 1/2 of your normal size, or even pause trading entirely to avoid unnecessary losses.

This proactive approach to adjusting position size keeps equity curve drawdowns under control while profits remain steadily growing.

## 4. Application Across Various Market Indices

This core logic applies to all major market indices, including A-shares, U.S. stocks, Hang Seng Index, and more. Regardless of market differences, the logic of contest at key levels and expectation confirmation remains consistent:

*   When the index clearly breaks through a key level and the expectation is confirmed, proactively enter the market;
*   When the index fails the contest at a key level or the expectation is unconfirmed, immediately execute your stop loss and exit to control risk.

## Core Concept

The essence of profitability is not about increasing the success probability of any single trade, but about keenly identifying market conditions and strictly executing position size management:

*   Clearly define key index levels and expectation confirmation standards;
*   When expectations are confirmed, attack aggressively; when expectations are unconfirmed, contract quickly.

Through precisely grasping market rhythm and disciplined position size management, you can achieve consistent profitability in long-term trading.
