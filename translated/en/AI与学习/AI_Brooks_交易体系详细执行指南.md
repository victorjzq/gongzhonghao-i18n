**I. Market Analysis: Determining Market Structure**
-----------------

**Task**: Determine the current market state and decide on a trading strategy.

### **1\. Determine the Current Market State**

1.  **Trend**


*   **Bull Trend**

    : Higher Highs (HH) + Higher Lows (HL).

*   **Bear Trend**

    : Lower Highs (LH) + Lower Lows (LL).

*   **Tight Channel**

    : A trend with almost no pullbacks.

*   **Broad Channel**

    : Large pullbacks, but the overall trend remains intact.


3.  **Trading Range (TR)**


*   Price moves **sideways** over a period, with highs and lows staying within a certain range.

*   **Buy Low, Sell High (BLSHS) strategy applies**

    , 80% probability of no breakout.


5.  **Breakout (BO)**


*   **Strong Breakout**

    (Strong BO): A large move up/down breaking through, with a Follow-through Bar.

*   **Failed Breakout (Failed BO)**

    : Price quickly reverses after the breakout and re-enters the trading range.

### **2\. Entry Details**

> **Core Principle: Wait for the signal bar + confirmation bar to ensure the breakout or pullback succeeds!**

#### **(1) Breakout Trade (BO)**

**Execution Steps**:

1.  Observe whether the market is in **Breakout Mode**:


*   Price has been in a trading range for **20+ bars**.

*   Price is near resistance/support and showing breakout intent (large bars).


3.  Identify the **Breakout Bar**:


*   **Big Body**

    , short upper/lower wicks, close near the high/low.

*   **Volume Increase**

    , indicating capital inflow.


5.  **Confirm the Breakout**

*   **After the breakout bar, the Follow-Through Bar must continue in the same direction**

    .

*   If the second bar retraces too deeply, it may be a Failed Breakout.


7.  **Execute Entry**

*   **Buy (Long)**

    : Place an order above the breakout bar's close + above the resistance level.

*   **Sell (Short)**

    : Place an order below the breakout bar's close + below the support level.


9.  **Stop Loss**

*   **Set stop loss below the breakout bar's low or below the most recent pullback level**

    .




* * *

#### **(2) Pullback Trade (PB Trade)**

**Execution Steps**:

1.  **Trend Established**

    :


*   Ensure the market has formed a clear trend (highs and lows forming a staircase pattern).

*   Identify key support levels (such as **EMA20, previous high, previous low**).


3.  **Pullback Confirmation**

    :


*   **Bull signal bar**

    : Long lower wick, body closes high.

*   **Bear signal bar**

    : Long upper wick, body closes low.


*   Observe price pulling back for **3-5 bars**.

*   **Long wicks on pullback bars indicate buyer/seller intervention**

    .

*   **A Signal Bar appears**

    :


6.  **Entry**

*   **Buy (Long)**

    : Enter 1 tick above the signal bar's high.

*   **Sell (Short)**

    : Enter 1 tick below the signal bar's low.


8.  **Stop Loss**

*   **Set stop loss below the signal bar's low or below the support level**

    .

**III. Risk Management: How to Control Losses?**
------------------

> **Objective**: Limit maximum loss per trade to 1%-2% of account equity.

### **1\. Capital Management**

*   Single trade **risk ≤ 2% of total account equity**.

*   **Maximum daily loss limit = 4-6% of account**

    , **stop trading** after exceeding the daily loss limit.


### **2\. Stop Loss Strategy**

Trade Type

Stop Loss Strategy

**Breakout Trade (BO Trade)**

Stop loss at **the breakout bar's low**

**Pullback Trade (PB Trade)**

Stop loss at **the pullback low (HL) or high (LH)**

**Trading Range Trade (TR Trade)**

Stop loss at **2-3 ticks outside the range**

**Reversal Trade**

Stop loss at **the reversal bar's low/high**

### **3\. Take Profit Strategy**

Trade Type

Take Profit Strategy

**Trend Trade**

**2x risk**

 or **Measured Move target**

**Pullback Trade (PB Trade)**

**Partial take profit at previous high/low**

, hold remaining position

**Trading Range Trade (TR Trade)**

**Take profit at the top/bottom of the range**

**Reversal Trade**

**Take profit at the previous high/low**
