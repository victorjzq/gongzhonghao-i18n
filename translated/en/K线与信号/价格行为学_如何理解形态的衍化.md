# Price Action: How to Understand the Evolution of Setups

I have been studying Price Action with Al Brooks for 2 years. Today is my 188th article, and you should also start learning Price Action.

When a sell signal or buy signal fails, if the signal itself was credible, it often leads to several legs in the opposite direction. For example, a failed Low 2 typically produces two legs up; a failed High 2 typically produces two legs down.
Therefore, traders must constantly ask themselves: What would it look like if a wedge bear flag succeeds? What would it look like if a wedge bull flag fails? And what is the next possible evolution?

A failed final flag reversal often evolves into a breakout-pullback pattern. The key is understanding how setups evolve, and what their next step might be after they complete. When a final flag reversal drops to the bottom of the flag, two scenarios may follow: either it develops into a Major Trend Reversal (MTR), with price continuing to fall to lower levels; or it evolves into a double bottom bull flag, followed by a breakout to the upside. Traders must constantly think about the next step and how these setups evolve.

When a High 2 evolves into a double top bear flag and breaks downward, producing two legs down, the entire structure may also evolve into a larger High 4. You can call it a large wedge bull flag, or you can call it a large High 4. But regardless of the name, the initial High 2 did not end the move — instead, it developed into a larger structure. The initial buy failure does not mean the entire flag has failed. It may simply be evolving into a larger setup, or preparing for an eventual reversal. The key is understanding this evolution process.

As another example, a Low 2 bear flag may evolve into a Low 3 wedge bear flag at the moving average (MA). If this setup also fails, the market may eventually see a failed breakout above the wedge. This creates two consecutive failures, ultimately evolving into a Low 5 sell. In other words, it gradually develops into a larger, more complex setup. If the market is testing near the moving average (MA) and is within a small bear channel, the expectation is still for a retest of the downside, unless the bulls can accelerate and successfully break out above the wedge.

Do not fixate on the success or failure of any single setup. Instead, focus on the evolution of setups and the probability bias provided by the context. Failed setups are often components of a larger structure.
