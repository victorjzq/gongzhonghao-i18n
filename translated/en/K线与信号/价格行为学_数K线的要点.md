# Price Action: Key Points of Bar Counting

High 1, 2, 3 are essentially failed reversals — after failing 1, 2, or 3 times, once price breaks above the highest point, the count restarts.

High 1, 2, 3 present certain patterns on the chart: double bottoms, wedges.



The essence of a pattern is the number of failed reversal attempts.

The number of times one side fails increases the win rate for the other side.

*   H1 (High 1): Once a bar breaks above the high of the previous bull bar during a pullback, it is an H1 buy setup;



*   H2 (High 2): If the first rally attempt fails and a second attempt forms a breakout, it is an H2 buy setup;



*   If bears fail twice consecutively in their attempts to push price down, they give up and exit, thereby driving the formation of an H2 bull flag;



*   Every double bottom structure can be viewed as an H2; if a triangle or wedge forms, it is an H3 structure.



When the third pullback (High 3) forms, if the structure slopes downward, it is a wedge bull flag; if the structure is roughly horizontal, it can be viewed as a triangle consolidation.



*   Buying pullbacks at H1, 2, 3 in a strong bull trend



*   Definition of a strong bull trend: Within the most recent 15 bars, preferably no more than 1 bar closes below the EMA



*   If a bull bar closes near its high, the probability of a successful breakout buy above it is higher.
