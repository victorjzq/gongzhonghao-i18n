# Precision Trading Guide: Structure, Signals, and Position Size Management

I. Trade Structure Analysis
========

### 1\. Expanding Triangle

*   **Structural Characteristics**

    : Price volatility gradually increases, with intense battle between bulls and bears.

*   **Key Operational Points**

    : Focus on volume, moving average (MA) support and resistance, and wait for a clear signal before taking action.


### 2\. Wedge

*   **Structural Characteristics**

    : The market fails three consecutive times pushing in the same direction (ascending wedge or descending wedge).

*   **Key Operational Points**

    : Watch for reversal signals when the third attempt fails, and prepare to enter.


### 2\. Head and Shoulders

*   **Structural Characteristics**

    : The neckline is the key level; breakouts or false breakouts at the neckline easily create trading opportunities.

*   **Key Operational Points**

    : After a neckline breakout, wait for the right shoulder pullback to fail on a second test before entering, to avoid traps.

II. Trading Signals and Trap Identification
-----------

### 1\. Three Pushes

*   After the market fails three consecutive times attempting to push to a top or bottom, a reversal move is likely to form.


### 2\. Failed Trap at the Top of an Expanding Triangle

*   Within a trading range, the second breakout at the top may appear strong, but it can easily turn into a false breakout and should be handled with caution.


### 3\. False Breakout Characteristics in Head and Shoulders Patterns

*   The first breakout of the neckline is often a false breakout; wait for the second retest confirmation before entering.

III. Precision Stop Loss and Take Profit Strategies
-----------

### Stop Loss Principles

*   Typically placed at key levels outside the trade structure, such as the extreme of a wedge or outside the neckline of a head and shoulders pattern.

*   Special Note: After a false breakout, react quickly and execute the stop loss strictly.


### Take Profit Strategies

*   **Scaling Out**

    : After reaching the initial target (1R, 2R), gradually reduce the position size;

*   **Dynamic Take Profit**

    : The remaining position can use dynamic profit protection to capture larger swing profits.

II. Key Trading Signals and Trap Identification
-------------

### 1\. Wedges and Triangles

*   The market fails three times touching the top or bottom (Three Pushes); only enter after confirmation.


### 2\. Nested Patterns and Trend Reversals

*   For example, a bear flag repeatedly probes lower without follow-through, followed by a successful bull breakout, forming a clear reversal entry signal.


### 3\. Head and Shoulders Breakout and Traps

*   After breaking the neckline of a head and shoulders pattern, always wait for the closing price to hold firmly above/below it.

*   If it is a false breakout, immediately prepare for a counter-trend trade.


### 4\. Breakout Confirmation and Failure Signals

*   A valid breakout requires "two closing confirmation bars" (breakout bar + follow-through bar).

*   Common false breakout patterns: "outside bull bar" or "long upper wick / shadow / tail".
