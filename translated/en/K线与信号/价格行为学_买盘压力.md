# Price Action: Buying Pressure

In trading, we must constantly assess: who has the upper hand, bulls or bears? One effective way is to observe **buying and selling pressure** in the market.

When the market shows **consecutive bull bars, almost no pullbacks, and bars closing near their highs**, it indicates that the bulls are in control. This force is what we call -- **buying pressure**.

* * *

### 1. What Is Buying Pressure?

*   Bulls are driving the market;

*   Institutions or active traders in the market exhibit **consistent buying behavior**;

*   Bulls don't wait for pullbacks -- they buy at the close, pushing prices higher.


**It typically appears in the following scenarios:**

*   A series of bull bars with large bodies;

*   Almost no overlap between consecutive bull bars;

*   Each bull bar closes near its high;

*   No significant pullbacks occur;

*   Bulls are clearly "chasing" the move higher;

*   Bars have long tails (lower wicks), but price is ultimately pulled back to the high, indicating bears are suppressed by bulls;

*   A bar's low is even higher than the previous bar's close or high, forming a "gap."


* * *

### 2. Specific Bar Characteristics of Buying Pressure

The following are common buying pressure signals. **Any single occurrence is worth noting, and multiple signals appearing together are even stronger**:

*   More bull bars than bear bars;

*   Consecutive bull bars with large bodies;

*   Bull bars closing near their highs, at least above the midpoint;

*   Bull microchannel: consecutive bull bars where each bar's low is above the previous bar's low;

*   Long lower tails on bars: indicates bears are being suppressed;

*   Gap structure: the current bar's low is above the previous bar's high;

*   Bull bars followed by more bull bars;

*   In the previous 20-30 bars, bull bars were scarce, then suddenly 4-5 consecutive bull bars appear;

*   Bull bar bodies are progressively getting larger;

*   The number of consecutive bear bars is decreasing, e.g., from 3 to 1-2;

*   Bear bar bodies are progressively getting smaller.


These are all manifestations of "active buying in the market."

* * *

### 3. What Is a "Bull Microchannel"?

A microchannel refers to:

*   Consecutive bull bars;

*   Each bar's low is **higher than or equal to the previous bar's low**;

*   Almost no pullbacks.


This is the strongest expression of buying pressure. It means:

*   Bulls don't wait for pullbacks -- they chase the move;

*   They enter at each close, seizing control;

*   If bars also have lower tails, it means bears attempted to push lower but failed, indicating strong bull momentum.


* * *

### 4. What Is a "Gap"?

A gap refers to:

*   The current bar's low is **higher than** the previous bar's high.


This "space" between prices indicates the market has jumped directly past the previous resistance zone.

**Gap manifestations include:**

*   The current bar's close > the previous high;

*   The current bar's low > the previous high;

*   A pullback bar's low > the high of the previous breakout bar.


This not only represents a **strong market breakout**, but also indicates:

*   The previous resistance has become support;

*   No one is willing to sell at this level;

*   The market must continue rising to find willing sellers.


* * *

### 5. The Relationship Between Gaps and Measured Moves

Gaps often foreshadow a **measured move up**.

**Example:**

*   The market breaks above a high, forming a gap;

*   After a pullback, it rallies again;

*   The rally distance is approximately equal to the pre-breakout rally (i.e., a "measured move").


Conversely, when a breakout fails (price falls back and **drops below the breakout point**):

*   Treat it as a "failed double bottom,"

*   And use the height of that breakout to **measure the downside target**.


* * *

### 6. Identifying Weakening Signals

When buying pressure is fading, the market will show:

*   Bull bars closing **below the previous high**;

*   **Strong bear bars** providing coverage;

*   Gaps being quickly closed.


**This indicates:**

*   Bull buying is decreasing;

*   The market is beginning to move sideways;

*   Or there is a risk of reversal.


###

### Understanding buying pressure helps you see the momentum behind trends, enabling higher-probability trading decisions.
