# In-Depth Guide to Bar/Candlestick Trading: From Signal Bar Interpretation to Handling Special Market Conditions

The key to successful trading is not blindly following indicators, but accurately identifying the battle between bulls and bears in the market. Today, we will walk through a clear trading path step by step, from the most basic bar/candlestick interpretation to advanced technical patterns and precise operations in special market conditions.
==============================================================================

* * *

## I. Preliminary Interpretation of Bar/Candlestick Signals

*   **Bar 1 (Bearish bar with no upper wick and a lower shadow/tail)**

*   Indicates that sellers temporarily have the initiative, with obvious selling pressure near the midpoint, suggesting an increased probability of a short-term pullback.

*   **Bar 2 (Opens above the previous close)**

*   The bulls' counter-attack intent is obvious, but it is still suppressed by the midpoint of the previous bar's body, and the bears still have the advantage.

*   **Bearish bar with a long shadow/tail**

*   Strong selling pressure appears but fails to close at the low, indicating the market is beginning to oscillate and may form a trading range.

* * *

## II. Identifying Market State: Bull-Bear Stalemate Phase

*   When the market enters a stalemate state, limit order trading characteristics are prominent, and it is difficult for the market to sustain a one-sided move.
*   Whenever the market attempts a one-directional breakout, counter-trend bars appear frequently, with clear oscillation characteristics.

* * *

## III. Strategies for the Trading Range Phase

*   **Obvious characteristics of a trading range**:

*   High breakout failure rate (up to 80%), weak bounce after breakout, continued oscillation.

*   **Trading range techniques**:

*   Sell short near the upper edge of the range, go long near the lower edge;
*   At the midpoint of the range (50% level), observe changes in bull-bear dynamics and adjust position size;
*   A pullback after a strong breakout (especially a 50% pullback) is a good with-trend entry point;
*   On a failed breakout, quickly trade in the opposite direction and lock in profits fast.

* * *

## IV. Special Bar/Candlestick Patterns and Practical Strategies

*   **Inside bullish bar after a strong bearish bar**:

*   If it does not break above the 50% level of the bearish bar's body, the bears still have the advantage — continue looking for shorts.

*   **Inverted Head and Shoulders Bottom**:

*   Confirmation requires the closing price to hold firmly above the neckline;
*   If the close fails to hold, it is a failed breakout — trade decisively in the opposite direction.

* * *

## V. Advanced Confirmation of Key Technical Patterns

*   **Double top confirmation principle**:

*   The closing price must effectively break below the neckline, and a second retest must fail before entry.

*   **Double bottom confirmation principle**:

*   Same method as the double top — patiently wait for neckline breakout or failure confirmation before taking action.

* * *

## VI. Refined Breakout Trading Techniques

*   **Breakout order placement technique**:

*   Place an order 1 tick below the key breakout level; if the breakout fails, immediately trade in the opposite direction.

*   **Quick reaction after a failed breakout**:

*   Promptly adjust trading direction to capture opportunities from reverse momentum.

* * *

## VII. Precise Handling of FOMC Special Market Conditions

*   **FOMC market characteristics**:

*   **Phase 1 (Statement release)**
    : Violent volatility easily forms failed breakouts — not advisable to enter blindly.

*   **Phase 2 (Press conference)**
    : The market becomes clearer, volatility has higher continuity, and it is more suitable for entry.

*   **Specific FOMC strategies**:

*   Be patient and observe, wait until the market structure is clear before entering;
*   Strictly distinguish between real and failed breakouts — if it fails, immediately reverse.

* * *

### Summary: A Clear Trading Loop

From bar/candlestick signals to market state, from trading ranges to special market conditions — handling trades requires maintaining dynamic thinking and strictly executing discipline. Gradually improve your understanding of the market and precisely seize every trading opportunity.
