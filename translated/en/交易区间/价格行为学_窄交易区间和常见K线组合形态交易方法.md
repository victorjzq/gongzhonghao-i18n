# Price Action: Tight Trading Range and Common Bar Pattern Trading Methods

## I. Tight Trading Range

**1. Do Not Rush to Trade**

In a tight trading range, if there have already been failed breakouts, the market will most likely continue to oscillate.

If 10 to 20 highly overlapping bars appear, it usually means the market will continue to move sideways.

**2. Limitations of Signal Bars**

Signal bars at the following positions are worth considering, provided the market context supports that direction:

- Bull signals appearing in the lower third of the range;
- Bear signals appearing in the upper third of the range.

---

## II. Structure Definitions and Classification

**3. Inside Bar (I), I-I, I-I-I Structures**

- Inside Bar: the current bar's high is not higher than the previous bar's, and both its high and low are contained within the previous bar.
- I-I: two consecutive inside bars.
- I-I-I / I-I-I-I: three or four consecutive inside bars, indicating extreme market compression and convergence.

**4. IOI (Inside-Outside-Inside) Structure**

- First bar: inside bar;
- Second bar: outside bar (high is higher than the previous bar, low is lower than the previous bar);
- Third bar: inside bar.

This indicates the market is building toward a breakout within a compression — a typical breakout mode structure.

**5. OIO (Outside-Inside-Outside) Structure**

Composed of outside bar - inside bar - outside bar. This reflects violent market oscillation without a clear direction, in a stage awaiting a breakout.

---

## III. Structural Patterns and Directional Judgment

**6. These structures themselves are not predictive — what matters is the position where they appear:**

- If they appear in the middle of a large trading range or at the tail end of a trend, they may be reversals or failed breakout traps;
- If they appear in a normal trading range, they are usually just continuation compressions.

**7. Entering rashly without a trending context = risky trade**

For example, after 5 consecutive down bars, a strong outside bull bar appears:

- If you go long directly, with a stop loss below the bar;
- The market continues to decline and your stop is hit;
- This type of trade is a typical bad trade.

---

## IV. Practical Applications in Special Scenarios

**8. Final Flag Reversal**

After a prolonged rally, an I-I or IOI structure appears, and the second bar is a bear bar:

- You can short below it;
- This belongs to a final flag reversal structure — a tradable setup.

**9. Judging Continuation vs. Reversal**

After a rally and pullback, an I-I structure appears with a relatively high success probability:

- The second bar is a bull bar with a close near its high;
- You can place a buy order above the high, treating it as a breakout test or High 2 bull flag.

**10. How to Judge Whether Context Is Good or Bad**

After a sustained rally, an extremely large bull bar appears, followed by an I-I-I structure:

- This type of large bull bar is often a trend-ending exhaustion bar;
- If the second bar of the I-I is a bear bar closing near its low, a reversal short is worth watching.

**11. Trend Inertia and Confirmation**

- If the market has been oscillating sideways for over 20 bars, the breakout direction of the I-I is nearly 50/50;
- If still in a trend with consecutive higher highs/lower lows, the I-I is more likely a normal pullback rather than a reversal.

**12. I-I Is a Triangle on a Smaller Timeframe**

When you see an I-I on a larger timeframe chart:

- You can switch to a smaller timeframe (e.g., from 60-minute to 5-minute);
- It usually presents as a converging triangle structure;
- The breakout direction determines the validity of the I-I.

**13. Trading Recommendations Within Trends**

In an uptrend:

- An I-I or IOI appears;
- The third bar has a strong bull body and price is above the moving average;
- You can place a long entry order above its high.

In a downtrend:

- An I-I or IOI appears;
- The third bar has a bear body and price is below the moving average;
- You can place a short entry order below its low.

---

## V. "Final Flag" and Reversal Signals at the End of Trading Ranges

---

- The market has been oscillating continuously for 15, 20, or more bars;
- Small structures like IOI, II, IIOI appear;
- The win rate is usually low — the market remains stagnant;

But if this structure appears at:

- **The tail end of an uptrend**;
- Or at **the high of a small channel**;
- Then this I-I or IOI may be a "final flag" and trigger a reversal.

Judgment method:

- Look at the structure on the left;
- Look for "a final push + failed follow-through";
- Look for a strong reversal bar following through.

Structure is not the signal — position is the key. Reading charts means reading the overall momentum.
