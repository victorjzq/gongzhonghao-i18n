# Price Action: Pullbacks and Trading Ranges

I. A Pullback Is a Pause in a Trend
Any pause within a trend is a pullback, and at the same time it is a small trading range. Traders expect the trend to resume. If the pullback extends to twenty bars or more, they begin to wonder: will the trend continue, or will it reverse?

II. Definition of a Pullback
"Pullback" implies that I expect the trend to resume. Whenever any bar in a trend drops below the low of the previous bar, that constitutes a pullback. Whether or not the low is breached, a single bear bar can be viewed as a pullback as long as it appears within a bull trend.

III. Expansion and Transition of Pullbacks
Pullbacks can expand. Once a pullback breaks below a "major low," traders no longer call it a bull trend — they say the market is now in a trading range or a bear trend.

IV. The Myth of the Perfect Trade
Many people believe they must wait for the "perfect entry" or the "perfect trade," but perfection does not exist in the market. Institutions are trading on every bar, and every bar carries substantial volume.

V. The Binary Decision Framework
Structuring trading decisions as binary choices makes them easier to judge. First ask yourself: is this a trend or a trading range? If it is a trend, is it a channel or a breakout? If it is a bull channel, is it tight or broad? Is the reversal minor or major? Narrow down the possibilities step by step through pairs of options.

VI. The Always-In Principle
Every market is either in a trend or in a trading range. If the market is in a trend, there must be an "always-in" direction. When you are always in the market, the best strategy is to hold a position in the direction of the current trend.

VII. Recognizing an Early Trading Range
When a trading range begins to form, you often feel confused — this is a hallmark characteristic. If you look at the chart and cannot tell what the market is doing, assume it is in the early stages of a trading range. Conversely, if price is simply climbing higher and you are still waiting for a pullback, the market is most likely in a trend.
