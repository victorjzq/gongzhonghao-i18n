# Price Action: How to Understand Trading Ranges (Part 10)

What is the probability of fading a strong second leg within a trading range?
This means you must confirm it is a trading range, you must confirm this is the second leg because you have already seen the first leg, and you want the second leg to be stronger.
So the key point is that you want the second leg to be stronger.

So you trade against the beginners.
Beginners see the second leg is strong and want to follow that direction.
But as a market maker or experienced trader, you say, wait — this is the second leg within a trading range, I think it will fail to break out, so I am going to trade against them.
So you are actually helping them enter a losing trade.
But you must be clear about your own trading logic.

The biggest problem is, as mentioned, most beginner traders only see trends and breakout moves.
They fail to realize that the market spends most of its time within trading ranges.

When judging second-leg traps, market structure is also critically important.
This requires a clear trading range and a strong second leg.

The trading range narrows and breakout attempts all fail.
When this happens three to four times — three times is enough to make a judgment, no need to wait for a fourth.
At this point, you should recognize that the market has entered breakout mode and pause all trading operations.
Wait for a clear market signal.
The market has entered a non-tradable zone.

When the channel is parallel to the moving average, you need to be extra careful.
Because a channel parallel to the moving average is one of the strongest types of tight channels, and they can persist for a very long time.
Corrections usually appear when there is excessive deviation — that is, when price has moved too far from the moving average, at which point you should expect a correction.
