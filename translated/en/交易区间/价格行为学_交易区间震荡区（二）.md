# Price Action: Trading Range (Part 2)

**I. A Series of Bull Bars Usually Means a Climax**
Multiple large bull bars forming a micro channel = buy climax.
Remember:
- One bull bar = a small breakout;
- A series of bull bars = a buy climax.

**II. A Strong Breakout After Twenty Bars Is More Likely Exhaustion**
When a trend has been running for approximately 20 bars and then a large bull breakout occurs, the higher probability is that this is the endpoint of exhaustion rather than the beginning of a new trend.
Exhaustion does not necessarily mean an immediate reversal into a bear trend; it only means the bulls no longer want to buy, and the market will most likely transition into a trading range or a deep pullback.

**III. Do Not Trade in Tight Ranges**
If after a big sell-off the market produces choppy small bars that overlap densely with many tails and dojis, and moves sideways for over 20 bars, neither bulls nor bears can profit.
The tighter the range, the more traders should stay on the sidelines and look for more active markets.

**IV. The "Paradox Logic" at Support and Resistance**
Many strong breakouts happen right at support/resistance levels. At these moments, betting on the breakout to fail is actually more profitable.
Even strong bulls can suddenly reverse — experienced traders use contrarian logic in trading ranges:
- In a trend, assume the breakout succeeds;
- In a range, assume the breakout fails.

**V. 80% of Breakout Attempts Fail**
Price does not stay long at the range top/bottom before reversing back to the middle; the most volume occurs in the middle, but the middle is the worst place to trade.
A better strategy: watch for bear breakout attempts, anticipate failure, then go long at the bottom of the range with at least a scalp-sized target.
Do not chase longs at the top — the probability of failure is far greater than success.

**VI. Detect Bull/Bear Imbalance Early**
Before a breakout, look for bar imbalances: for example, 5-6 consecutive bear bars, increasing lower highs and lower lows, indicating growing selling pressure.
Even if the overall structure is a pullback within an uptrend, if the pullback evolves into a trading range and bear bars outnumber bull bars = bears are slightly stronger.
Traders aim to test the waters with small positions before the real breakout.

**VII. The Universal Ending**
All trends and all channels eventually become trading ranges.
In trending phases, bet on success; in range phases, bet on failure.
When the direction is unclear, step aside first, wait for the structure to speak, then follow or reverse.
