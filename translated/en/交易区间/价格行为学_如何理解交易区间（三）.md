So what are the basic conditions of a trading range?

Obviously, it is two moves in opposite directions.

But there are other conditions — when you do not see an opposing move yet determine that a trading range is forming, meaning this is a leg within a trading range.

Identifying this leg as a component of a trading range.

You know, in many trading ranges during the formation process, there is no chance to form a counter-trend move because the first leg fails and the market breaks out.

Therefore, you will see within a trading range a move like this: there are bull bars, followed by weak inside bars, bars with upper tails, and similar characteristics.

The patterns of these bars do not really fit the characteristics of a trending market.

The market shows a strong uptrend.

There are no effective sell signals at this stage.

Then a micro channel pattern appears in the direction opposite to the uptrend.

This type of situation usually does not occur in a bull trend, but happens within trading ranges.

Therefore, there is usually selling pressure above the top.

In most cases, the market will first probe the area above the top, and then form a trading range.
