# Price Action: How to Trade — The Complete Progression from Breakout to Trading Range

**1. Identify the Breakout**

- Look for 2-3 consecutive large bull bars with full bodies, closing near their highs.

- Preferred context: (1) A breakout at the end of a broad "three-push" pattern; (2) A breakout at the end of a sideways trading range (TR).

- Avoid chaotic structures of sharp drop-sharp rally-sharp drop; the more gradual the decline, the more worthwhile the subsequent upward breakout.

**2. Entry Methods**

- Market order follow-through: chase the trade at the close of each strong bull bar (follow-through).

- Limit buy at the low: place a buy limit order at the low of each large bull bar, betting on "the first pullback to fail."

**3. Position Management After the First Pullback**

- Once a pullback appears, reduce part of the position as planned to lock in profits.

- Keep the core position, continue holding with the trend, entering the normal trend-following rhythm.

**4. H2 / H3 Strategy During Consecutive Pullbacks**

- When multiple consecutive pullback bars appear, watch for H2 (two-leg) or H3 patterns:

- H2 succeeds -> expect the main trend to continue, set targets using 1:1 measured move (MM).

- H2 / H3 fails -> adopt a conservative strategy of scaling in, measuring, and breaking even, exiting defensively in time.

**5. Identifying and Responding to Trading Range Signals**

- Conditions: pullback >= 20 bars, or a violent sell-off after the third leg up.

- Response: treat it as a trading range (TR), take profits near the prior high; switch order types from stop orders to limit orders, buy low and sell high.

**6. Two Key Scenarios in the Trading Range**

(1) Downward false breakout (failed double bottom)
- Identify it as a failed range pattern, exit or reverse based on measured move; still use MM scaling in to break even, avoid being deeply trapped.

(2) Two large bull bars closing consecutively at the bottom and reclaiming the midline ("failure of failure")
- Go long from the bottom of the range, target holding for 2R-3R.

---

**Core Takeaways**

- Good context + strong momentum is the foundation for breakout success rates.

- The first pullback is the watershed: if it fails, the uptrend accelerates; if it succeeds, the pace slows.

- Once momentum fades, immediately switch to trading range thinking, using limit orders to "buy low and sell high" at the range edges.

By executing in layers according to this logic, you can extract opportunities from the breakout, pullback, and trading range phases, achieving a dynamic balance of risk and reward.
