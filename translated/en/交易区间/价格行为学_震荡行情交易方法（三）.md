# Price Action: Trading Range Methods (Part 3)

Whenever a pattern has a high probability within a trend
and you see that same pattern in a trading range,
the probability is not as high as you think —
it may be closer to 50% rather than 60%.

All the signs that indicate the market is in a trading range
come from how traders are trading.
Traders believe that selling will not push prices down very far,
so they do not want to sell at the bottom,
while bulls believe that sell-offs and bear breakouts will fail.
They are betting the market will reverse upward.
Then, vice versa —
when the market approaches prior highs, bears are more willing to sell the rally,
but they are not willing to hold their shorts for long
because they do not believe the downward reversal will last very far.
And bulls are not willing to buy at the highs —
they are looking for opportunities to sell the rally
to cash in on their profits from buying at the lows.
Bulls buy low and sell high,
bears sell high and buy low.
Therefore, both bulls and bears are buying low and selling high,
and both are taking quick profits.
The result is a trading range, and this is why when the market is in a trading range,
traders should look to buy low, sell high, and scalp —
taking quick profits.
Bulls are actually buying bear reversals,
betting the reversal will fail.
Therefore, strong bear bars —
many bulls will buy them if they believe this is
just a trading range.
They will also buy reversal rallies.
And when the market approaches the top, bears will do the opposite.

As more bars are added, the trading range can expand,
you get a new upper half and lower half,
and traders will operate just as before.

Stop order entries are bets on breakouts and trends.
When the market is in a tight trading range,
rather than using stop orders to buy at the top and betting on a trend,
it is better to sell at the top with limit orders,
betting that the trading range will continue.
And rather than using stop orders to sell at the bottom and betting on a trend forming,
it is better to buy at the bottom with limit orders,
betting the breakout fails and reverses back into the trading range.
When the trading range is very tight,
most traders should not trade.

Stop orders are bets on the start of a trend,
while limit orders are bets on a reversal rather than a trend.
