# Price Action Trading System Manual (4)

The only measure of successful learning = whether you can replicate the author's reasoning sequence

The market makes two attempts to break out.
---------

The first fails, and so does the second.

Patterns are only the beginning. Beginners see a double top and sell, see a double bottom and buy, then get stopped out in a strong trend.

They chase perfect patterns, but the market is never perfect.

### Double Top = Market fails twice to push higher

Price rallies, pulls back, then rallies again to test the prior high. The second high does not need to align perfectly. It can be slightly higher (Higher High) or slightly lower (Lower High).

### Double Bottom = Market fails twice to push lower

Price drops, bounces, then drops again to test the prior low. The second low can be slightly higher (Higher Low) or slightly lower (Lower Low).

Double top neckline: the lowest point between the two highs

Double bottom neckline: the highest point between the two lows

A breakout of the neckline confirms the pattern; the distance from the neckline to the top/bottom determines the target.

### Micro double top/bottom:

An extremely short-term double top or bottom forming within 2–5 bars.

---

In a strong trend,
-----

Bull trend: wait for a double top to fail, forming a double bottom, then place a buy order above a bullish signal bar

Bear trend: wait for a double bottom to fail, forming a double top, then place a sell order below a bearish signal bar

To trade reversals, you need to see:

The pattern is part of a Major Trend Reversal (MTR), a strong opposing signal bar appears (large body, breaking a key level), and the risk-reward ratio is at least 2:1.

In a trading range:

Sell a double top at the top of the range, buy a double bottom at the bottom of the range, risk-reward ratio 1:1

### The biggest beginner mistake

Selling immediately on a double top, buying immediately on a double bottom. Waiting for a second entry formed by a micro double top or double bottom has a higher win rate.

Place the stop loss beyond the high/low of the pattern. If price breaks through that level, your pattern is invalid. Accept the mistake and exit immediately.

### Double top failure (breakout above the high)

The market breaks above the high of the double top but then reverses down. This often forms a wedge top (three pushes). Exit immediately with a stop loss and look for a wedge reversal opportunity instead.

### Double bottom failure (break below the low)

The market breaks below the low of the double bottom but then reverses up. This often forms a wedge bottom (three pushes). Exit immediately with a stop loss and look for a wedge reversal opportunity instead.

### Failure of the failure

Both sets of double tops/double bottoms fail.

Enter on two consecutive trend bars in the same direction, targeting 2R or more (this is swing trading).

---

In a tight channel (extremely strong trend), double top/double bottom reversals usually fail.

Because the trend is too strong, pullbacks are just flags.

This is the number one reason beginners lose money.
