**Special Patterns and Advanced Concepts**
-------------

I1R Pattern (Initial 1R) — refers to a leg initiated by a reversal bar whose magnitude reaches the 1x risk-reward (1R) target calculated from the risk of that signal bar.

    Step 1: Reversal occurs — a strong reversal bar appears at a support/resistance level

    Confirms market imbalance

IOI Pattern (Inside-Outside-Inside) — three consecutive bars,

Pattern: inside bar - outside bar - inside bar

    Bar 2 engulfs Bar 1

On a lower timeframe: = expanding triangle + converging triangle; the market is in an extreme breakout mode

Pattern characteristics:

    Bar 1: inside bar

Market psychology:

    Inside bar → hesitation

The market is making its final decision

    1. Wait for the breakout — the market is in a state of hesitation; wait for price to break the boundary of the pattern

#### **Always In concept — the market is in a state of clear one-sided control such that entering at market price (Long or Short) at the current price is mathematically justified.**

####

#### **Price Action Determination**

    Strong breakout bar

Moving average determination

    Price above the moving average (EMA) → Always In Long

When a decisive breakout occurs, the market officially enters the Always In state

    Significantly far from the moving average

Once the Always In direction is determined, only trade in that direction

Always In Long: only go long, never short

Always In Short: only go short, never long

During Always In Long phase: ignore weak bearish signals; you can even: place a buy stop below a bear signal bar (anticipating a failed reversal)

Only change the Always In direction when the following occurs:

    Strong opposing breakout

Opening Strategy (The Open)

15-30 minutes before the open, Reconnaissance phase — trading is generally not recommended; use this time to gather information

    Size of the opening gap

Opening Range — the range formed by price movement during the first 30-60 minutes after the open; the highest and lowest points after the open form the boundaries of the opening range.

Opening Range Breakout (ORBO) — price strongly breaks the boundary of the opening range.

    Breakout upward: if price strongly breaks through the top of the opening range — go long

Double strong bar open — two consecutive strong trend bars in the same direction at the open; typically sets the tone for the day:

    Trend Day

Trade in the direction of the double strong bars; expect that direction to continue; do not trade against it

Intraday trading time windows — first hour after the open (9:30-10:30 ET): observe primarily, trade cautiously.

    Highest volatility

Mid-morning (10:30-11:30): if a trend has been established, this is the optimal trading window

    Volatility decreasing

Midday session (11:30-13:00): avoid trading, or scalp the range only

    Liquidity decreasing

Afternoon session (13:00-15:00): watch for measured move targets or reversal signals

    Liquidity recovering

Pre-close (15:00-16:00): experienced traders may trade; beginners should avoid (high unpredictability)

    Volatility increases again
