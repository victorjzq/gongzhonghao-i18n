# Price Action Trading System Manual (1)

The only measure of successful learning = whether you can reproduce the author's reasoning sequence.

Trading is a math game. Institutional algorithms seek target levels based on geometric symmetry. Successful trading comes from correctly identifying the market environment and executing a strategy that matches it.

To constrain trading illusions and avoid premature speculation, I choose to first look at the direction of the signal bar, then check the three elements to assess trade quality and decide whether to trade.

The three elements are: context, pattern, and location.

Context is the most complex element — an abstract concept that requires other concepts to define it.

## Market Environment

Is the current market in a bullish environment, bearish environment, or neutral environment?

The simplest and most effective method is to observe the relationship between price and the Exponential Moving Average (EMA). Price above the MA with the MA sloping upward indicates a bullish environment.

## Market Behavior

Is the current market trending or in a trading range?

### Trend

| Characteristic | Behavior |
|---|---|
| Bar continuity | Consecutive strong trend bars with almost no overlap |
| MA condition | EMA visibly sloping |
| Pullback depth | Pullbacks are shallow and brief |
| Price structure | Continuously making new highs/lows |
| Gaps | Gaps remain unfilled |

A microchannel is the hallmark of a strong trend:

> "A microchannel is a series of bars with no pullback. Bull microchannel: each bar's low is equal to or higher than the previous bar's low."

### Trading Range

| Characteristic | Behavior |
|---|---|
| Bar overlap | Large amount of overlap between bars |
| MA condition | EMA flattening, price oscillating around the EMA |
| Two-way trading | Both bulls and bears can profit |
| Frequent dojis | More indecisive bars |
| Failed breakouts | Breakout attempts quickly reverse |

## Determining the Always In Direction

Always In means: if you had to enter right now and hold a position in one direction, which direction would you choose? It helps traders filter out the impulse to trade counter-trend. This is a forced binary thinking tool that removes the "wait/neutral" option.

The primary purpose of identifying Always In is to filter out incorrect trade directions. If the market is in an Always In Long state, you should remove "going short" from your decision tree and only look for buying opportunities. It also eliminates hesitation — when Always In Long, you should buy even if it "feels too high" — and serves as a basis for holding: as long as the state doesn't change, continue holding.

Always In = judge whether bulls or bears can more easily profit right now, then only follow that side.

```
State:    Always In Long
Meaning:  Bulls control the market, going long is easier to profit
Features: Consecutive large bull bars, breakout to new highs, bear counterattacks suppressed
────────────────────────────
State:    Always In Short
Meaning:  Bears control the market, going short is easier to profit
Features: Consecutive large bear bars, break below support, bull rallies fail

Simple determination method — ask yourself: "Who can more easily profit right now?"

Bulls can profit by buying      → Always In Long  → only go long
Bears can profit by selling     → Always In Short → only go short
Both can profit / neither can   → Direction unclear → wait
```

## Always In Determination Method (Specific Execution Criteria)

**Step 1: Look at bar characteristics**

| Feature | Always In Long | Always In Short |
|---|---|---|
| Bar body | Large bull bar, close near high | Large bear bar, close near low |
| Bar overlap | Little overlap between consecutive bars | Little overlap between consecutive bars |
| Relation to MA | Closing above the 20 EMA | Closing below the 20 EMA |
| Gap | Low higher than previous bar's high | High lower than previous bar's low |

**Step 2: How many bars are needed for confirmation?**

| Situation | Bars Required |
|---|---|
| Standard confirmation | 2 bars: breakout bar + follow-through bar (both strong trend bars in the same direction) |
| Conservative confirmation | 3 bars: 3 consecutive trend bars in the same direction |
| High confidence confirmation | 4 bars: 4 consecutive same-direction bars all closing on one side of the MA |
| Extreme case | 1 bar: a huge surprise bar (but it is still recommended to wait for follow-through confirmation) |

**Step 3: Checklist**

Before determining Always In, check each item:

- [ ] 1. Are there large-body trend bars? (Close near the extreme)
- [ ] 2. Does the next bar go in the same direction? (Not a doji or a reversal bar)
- [ ] 3. Are the bars closing on one side of the 20 EMA?
- [ ] 4. Has a key level been broken? (Range boundary / prior high or low)
- [ ] 5. Are counter-trend traders trapped? (No pullback allowing them to exit at breakeven)

4 or more out of 5 satisfied → Always In can be confirmed

## Specific Bar Combination Examples

**Always In Long (go long)**

Example 1: Breakout + follow-through
```
Bar 1: Large bull bar, close near the high
Bar 2: Another bull bar, closing above Bar 1's high
→ Always In Long confirmed
```

Example 2: Three consecutive bull bars
```
Bar 1: Bull bar
Bar 2: Bull bar, closing higher
Bar 3: Bull bar, closing higher
→ Always In Long confirmed
```

Example 3: Breakout above MA
```
Bar 1: Large bull bar breaks out and closes above the 20 EMA
Bar 2: Bull bar, remains above the MA
→ Always In Long confirmed
```

**Always In Short (go short)**

Example 1: Consecutive bear bars
```
Bar 1: Large bear bar
Bar 2: Large bear bar
Bar 3: Bear bar, closing below the 20 EMA
→ Always In Short confirmed
```

Example 2: Failed reversal
```
Bar 1: Bull bar (bull attempt)
Bar 2: A larger bear bar engulfs the bull bar
→ Always In Short confirmed
```

Example 3: Breakout + follow-through
```
Bar 1: Large bear bar breaks out below the range low
Bar 2: Another bear bar
→ Always In Short confirmed
```

## Simplified Decision Flow

Upon seeing a large trend bar, wait for the next bar:

```
├── Same-direction strong bar → Always In confirmed → only trade this direction
│
└── Doji / reversal bar → Not confirmed → wait or treat as chop
```

**One-sentence summary:** Large trend bar + same-direction follow-through bar + closing on one side of the MA = Always In established

## When Does the Always In Direction Change?

1. A strong trend bar appears in the opposite direction
2. Consecutive bars closing in the opposite direction
3. A prior gap is closed
4. After breaking a trendline, a test of the extreme fails

If you incorrectly judge that going short is viable while the market is in an Always In Long regime, you will typically either capture only a tiny scalp profit or face an outright loss.
