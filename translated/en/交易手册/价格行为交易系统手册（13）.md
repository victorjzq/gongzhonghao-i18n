# Price Action Trading System Manual (13)

Every bar is either a trend bar or a trading range bar — it must be one of the two.

A trend bar (Trend Bar) has a body that occupies most of the bar's price range, indicating that one side has complete control.

    Body percentage: ≥50%; ideally 2/3 or more

A trading range bar (Trading Range Bar) has a small body with long upper and lower wicks, indicating balanced forces between bulls and bears.

    Body percentage: <30%–50%

Gap Theory

    Gap = Breakout = Spike = Simple Leg

Micro gap: no overlap between the high (or low) of the prior bar and the low (or high) of the following bar. Typically refers to a gap between Bar 1's high and Bar 3's low.

    Strong gap

    Weak gap

Tick gap: the prior bar's close ≠ the current bar's open (at least 1 tick of space)

    Extreme urgency

Body gap: no overlap between bar body sections (wicks may overlap)

    Switch to "Line on Close" chart

Negative gap: the current bar's open gaps in the opposite direction back into the prior bar's close (overlap occurs)

    Momentum weakening

Opening gap: the difference between the current day's open and the prior day's close (or Globex overnight session)
