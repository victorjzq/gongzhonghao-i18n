# Trading Book Series: 12 "The New Market Wizards"

This book is a collection of interviews with multiple top traders.

Average trader: See profit potential → Enter the market → Figure out what to do when things go wrong
Top trader: **Evaluate and lock in maximum loss → Plan the escape route → Patiently wait for market confirmation signals → Force calm, then execute precisely**

### Confirm Market Direction, Never Guess Subjectively

**Richard Driehaus**: "I won't buy a stock when it's dropping even if I like the fundamentals. I have to see some stability in the price action before I buy the stock."

**Randy McKay**: "I never try to buy a bottom or sell a top... You want to wait until the move is already under way before you get into the market."

### Preset Risk and Exit Strategy (Defense First)

**Victor Sperandeo**: "When I first look at a trade, I don't ask, 'What is the potential profit I can realize?' but rather, 'What is the potential loss I could suffer?'"

**Bill Lipschutz**: "Before I put on a trade, I always ask myself, 'If this trade goes wrong, how do I get out?'"

### Strictly Limit Position Size

**Monroe Trout**: "At the beginning of each month, I determine the maximum position size that I'm willing to take in each market, and I don't exceed that limit, regardless of how bullish or bearish I get."

### Mental Rehearsal and Pre-Execution Restraint

**Tom Basso**: "I mentally live through what will happen to my positions, given one of these scenarios... It helps prepare me for all the different market conditions that can arise."

**Linda Bradford Raschke**: "I try to wait until things set up just right before I take a trade. Then, when I'm ready to take the trade, I slowly count to ten before I pick up the phone."

You must preset exit points before entering a position.



**Victor Sperandeo**: "Losses are always predetermined so that I can measure my risk."

**Never Harbor False Hope (Abandon "Hope")**

"Hope" is a dirty word in trading. Once you determine a position is wrong, act immediately — do not wait for a better price that may never come.

**Maintain Absolute "Disloyalty" to Positions (Learn to be disloyal)**

**Stanley Druckenmiller's 1987 Case**: The day before the crash, he mistakenly switched from short to 130% leveraged long. After realizing his error over the weekend, he immediately liquidated the long position at Monday's open and reversed to short, avoiding disaster.

### Edge — Do Not Confuse It with Luck. Trading Without an Edge Is Gambling

"If you don't have an edge, all that money management and discipline will do for you is to guarantee that you will gradually bleed to death. Incidentally, if you don't know what your edge is, you don't have one."

### The Temptation of Counter-Trend Trading

**William Eckhardt**: "A constellation of cognitive and emotional factors that makes people automatically countertrend in their approach."

People want to buy low and sell high. They tend to view familiar prices as "normal," and once prices deviate, they consider it abnormal — leading them to trade against the trend right when it begins.

Refusing to Cut Losses and Taking Profits Too Early
-----------

**Victor Sperandeo**: "The single most important reason that people lose money in the financial markets is that they don't cut their losses short."

**William Eckhardt**: "While amateurs go broke by taking large losses, professionals go broke by taking small profits."

### Oversized Positions (Excessive Leverage)

**Mark Ritchie**: "Controlling position size is indispensable to success. Of all the traits necessary to trade successfully, this factor is the most undervalued."

### Trying to Pick Tops and Bottoms

**Randy McKay**: "I never try to buy a bottom or sell a top."

The mistake of losers: "They try to put their own opinion of what will happen before the market action."

### Trading for "Excitement"

**Mark Ritchie**: "If you get a buzz from profits and depressed by losses, you belong in Las Vegas, not the markets."

### Seeking Opinions from Others

**Linda Raschke**: "If you ever find yourself tempted to seek out someone else's opinion on a trade, that's usually a sure sign that you should get out of your position."

### Flawed Conventional Wisdom

**Richard Driehaus against "Buy Low, Sell High"**: "I believe that far more money is made buying high and selling at even higher prices."

**Victor Sperandeo against "Buy and Hold"**: "The greatest misconception is the idea that if you buy and hold stocks for long periods of time, you'll always make money."

Trend Identification Methods
------

### The Three-Step Breakout Method (Victor Sperandeo)



1\. Wait for the uptrend line to be broken

2\. Look for an unsuccessful test of the recent high

3\. A downward penetration of the most recent relative low = trend change confirmation



> In a bull trend, continuously draw trend lines; the first break below is often just a bull flag.
>
>
>
> If the subsequent test of the old high fails and a lower high / double top appears, the probability of a bearish opportunity increases.

The Role of the Subconscious (Robert Krausz)
---------------------

1. **Change Negative Language**: Don't say "don't think about money" — say "focus on following your method"

2. **Use Anchoring to Eliminate Fear Memories**: Turn painful images into still photographs, accompanied by comical music

3. **Emotional Dissociation**: Imagine stepping back and observing the trading version of yourself

**The Jade Cutter Parable**

A young man sought to learn jade identification from a jade cutter. Each time, the jade cutter placed a green stone in his hand and then told various stories. Until one spring, the young man suddenly said: "The stone I hold is not genuine Jade."

True market intuition cannot be taught through shortcuts. It must be gained through prolonged immersion, observation, and firsthand experience. When you have accumulated enough experience, your intuition will naturally distinguish opportunity from trap.
