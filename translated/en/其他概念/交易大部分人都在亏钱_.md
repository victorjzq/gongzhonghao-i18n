# Are Most Traders Losing Money?

Some studies say about 95% of day traders lose money, and they would be better off driving for a ride-sharing service. What do you think?

AL: Similar "statistics" have always existed, but the problem is they usually include everyone — including people who treat trading purely as gambling or a hobby. It's like how most people who play soccer don't make money from it, because it's just entertainment. If you only look at those who are committed, systematically invest time in studying, and take trading seriously, the loss rate is far lower than that. Also, combining short-term and long-term trading in the statistics yields completely different conclusions. If you buy quality stocks and hold them long-term, you'll most likely make money. Day trading is just one of many approaches, but many people start by trying to scalp on 1-minute or 5-minute charts, which is actually riskier and requires a much higher skill level.

Reflection:

Trading is fundamentally the same as any other profession:

**Treat it casually, and it's gambling;
Treat it seriously, and it's a career.**

A professional attitude, systematic training, and long-term focus
are the only path to consistent profitability.
