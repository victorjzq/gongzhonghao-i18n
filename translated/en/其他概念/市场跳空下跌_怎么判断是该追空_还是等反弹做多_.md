# Market Gaps Down: How to Judge Whether to Chase the Short or Wait for a Bounce to Go Long?

The key lies in one thing: Do you understand what type of gap this is?

Don't rush to act — first distinguish between three critical types of gaps: **breakaway gap, measuring gap, and exhaustion gap.**

---

## I. Breakaway Gap: Signal of Trend Initiation

**Characteristics:**
- Appears at the beginning of a trend
- The gap drops rapidly with volume, not filled

This indicates bears are taking control and the trend is officially established.

**Strategy:**
- Wait for price to retest the prior low or gap edge to go short
- Target set at 1x the gap height as the first profit-taking level
- Stop loss above the gap — if invalidated, exit

**One-sentence summary:** If the gap isn't filled, don't fantasize about a reversal.

---

## II. Measuring Gap: Signal of Trend Continuation

What many people most frequently get wrong is the "measuring gap."

This is a gap that appears in the middle of a trend, marking trend acceleration.

**Typical pattern:**
- After a breakout, a pullback (PB) forms
- A gap opens between the PB and the breakout point
- Bulls/bears refuse to allow a retracement, showing the dominant side controls the pace
- The gap remains unfilled, indicating strong momentum

**Strategy:**
- After the measuring gap appears, wait for price to retrace to the gap edge or support level, then enter with the trend
- Target based on equal-length measured move from the prior trend leg
- For example, if the prior leg rose 20 points, the post-breakout move may also rise 20 points

**Special reminder:**
- Measuring gaps should not be quickly filled
- If the gap gets filled, it may not be "measuring" but "exhaustion"
- At that point, you must stop out — don't fight it

**One-sentence summary:** An unfilled gap means the dominant side isn't giving the opponent a chance; a gap that gets filled means the momentum isn't as strong.

---

## III. Exhaustion Gap: Warning of Trend Ending

**Characteristics:**
- Appears at the end of a prolonged trend
- Initially produces a gap, but gets quickly filled within 1-3 bars

This is the classic "last gasp."

**Operating logic:**
- Wait for the gap to close, then confirm a reversal pattern (such as a hammer or morning star)
- When consecutive bull bars appear, try a light counter-trend long position
- Stop loss below the prior low to control risk

**One-sentence summary:** A real trend doesn't look back; one that does may be at the end.

---

## IV. Read the Gap, Decide What to Do

How to judge whether to trade with the trend or prepare to reverse?

Look at two key indicators:

**1. Whether the gap is filled**
- Not filled: continue with the trend
- Filled quickly: reduce shorts or even reverse

**2. Bar strength**
- If the bounce is weak (long upper tails, small bodies), the dominant side hasn't let go
- If a strong bull engulfing bar appears, the market may have switched sides — pull back

---

**Summary of Operating Path**
- Breakaway gap → Retrace and short, take profits at measured target
- Measuring gap → Follow pullback, take profits at equal-distance extension
- Exhaustion gap → Gap filled + structural reversal, close shorts and try long

---

**Gaps aren't accidental — they're intentional**

Most people only see "a gap," but don't see who's behind the force.

A measuring gap is the market saying: "I'm not done yet."
An exhaustion gap is the market saying: "I can't go any further."

What you need to do is hear that message, then follow it.

**Read the gap, match the rhythm, and one trade beats ten.**
