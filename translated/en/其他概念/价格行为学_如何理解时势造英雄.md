# Price Action: How to Understand "The Times Make the Hero"

The same 5-minute breakout strategy produces vastly different results depending on the 1-hour context.

The market is fractal.

If the 1-hour chart has just completed a moving average breakout and price is pulling away, pushing forward, then a 5-minute upward breakout is more likely to follow through.

If the 1-hour chart has formed a wedge with three pushes and then given a signal bar, the market has an expectation of a rally.

When you wait on the 5-minute chart for an upward breakout, you're actually participating in a segment where the larger timeframe pattern is being realized.

"The times make the hero" — essentially, the era has already paved the road, and the individual simply stepped onto the right node.

**Breakouts don't happen out of thin air.** They happen on a premise: the underlying conditions have already accumulated to the point where the system has entered the "ready to break out" phase.

Resources, technology, institutions, supply and demand, demographics, capital — these things build up at the foundational level first. The surface still looks calm, but the structure has already changed, resistance is decreasing, and channels are opening up.

At this point, the same action, the same move, suddenly has a much higher success rate, and the pace of progress noticeably accelerates.

**Making the right move near the critical point**, seizing a breakout opportunity. **The environment has already allowed this breakout to sustain and to realize a larger space.**

You can focus on participating in the segment of the higher timeframe's signal bar move.
