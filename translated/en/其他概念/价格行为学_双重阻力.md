# Price Action: Double Resistance

When a **wedge structure** and a **support/resistance line form a confluence**, the pattern is truly complete.

In this structure, the first **buy limit order** can be placed at the trendline (or channel line).

When trading so-called **"double-line patterns"**, pay special attention to the path of the final push. If price must **first break through the channel line before it can reach the resistance line above**, then the more reasonable approach is:

- Execute a first sell or partial exit near the channel line;
- Simultaneously place a second limit order above the resistance line in advance.

If price not only touches the resistance line but **clearly breaks above it with a breakout bar**, this indicates that the "double-line pattern" has failed and the structure should be considered broken.

When the first entry occurs at a **position with excessive momentum** — which is inherently a low-probability trade — the trader can choose to **simply pass** or participate with only a **very light position and no additional scaling in**.

If after buying at that position, you find there is **insufficient profit room above** and **no next clear, reasonable scaling-in price level exists**, you should not force yourself to hold — just exit the position.

From a more macro perspective, **trading range price action tends to break through the first key price level but rarely breaks through the second consecutively**. Therefore, if the "double-line pattern" ends near the first level, a brief breakout of that level is acceptable, and the **second batch-entry limit order** can be placed **above the second resistance** to wait for better structural confirmation.
