# Price Action: When Should You Wait

When the market shows a possible major trend reversal signal, you can think about it this way: I won't act rashly until there has been one successful short-term trade in the original trend direction. This approach helps you avoid prematurely betting on trend resumption and getting caught in a failed reversal trade.

The key is: wait for the bears' or bulls' strength to re-establish itself and complete at least one successful short-term trade. This shows the market is willing to accept the new direction, rather than it being just a single false breakout.

You should first identify the structure, then look for swings within the structure, rather than using swing counts to define the structure. Because if you start with swing counts, you're easily influenced by subjective judgment, losing your understanding of the market's overall structure.

Structure is a pattern that has already formed — it tells you what the market is doing. Identifying swings within the structure helps you more clearly determine whether the market is currently in a trend or has reached the end of a trend.

Many traders confuse these two. They keep counting swings, trying to predict reversals through swing counts. The result is they constantly attempt counter-trend trades and repeatedly get punished by the market.

This mistake is especially easy to make in channel patterns. A channel's dominant direction usually pushes three times, while the counter-direction may push twice. Once you see these five waves completed, the safest approach is to stop trading and enter a waiting mode.

For example, you see the market seems about to break out, then a first leg up appears, followed by a second and third leg. In between, there are two pullbacks — perhaps two distinct down legs. At this point, you really shouldn't have too many expectations for the trend to continue.

This is a "stand aside" signal. You don't necessarily need to immediately reverse and go short, but you can choose to stop going long with the trend.

What you do next is wait. Sometimes it's one or two bars, sometimes it's one or two hours. As long as the channel has experienced five touches, that's a signal that the trading rhythm should pause. Even if the structure between these touches is complex, you must respect the market's rhythm.

You also need to consider the issue of trading room. If this structure appears in a high-price zone, it means your profit potential is very limited. Entering recklessly at this point means the risk clearly outweighs the reward.

If you've done sufficient preparation, have clear expectations for how the market will develop, and know exactly "what signal represents failure," then once that failure signal appears, don't hesitate — exit immediately.

Because it has already deviated from your expectation.

This is like trust between people. Someone has a good reputation and you trust them. But suddenly they do something that betrays your trust. Going forward, if you need to continue trusting them, you'll naturally think: "Wait, I feel you're no longer as trustworthy as before."
