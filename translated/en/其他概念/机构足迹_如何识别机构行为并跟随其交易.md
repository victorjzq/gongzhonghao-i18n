# Institutional Footprints: How to Identify Institutional Behavior and Follow Their Trades

In financial markets, institutional trading behavior typically dominates the market's major price movements. Therefore, if a trader can identify institutional moves, they can capture more trading opportunities in the market.

---

## 1. Institutional Footprints: Price Movement Is Dominated by Institutional Behavior

Price movement is not entirely caused by retail behavior. Institutional investors typically control most of the market's capital flow, and their trading behavior has a profound impact on price movement. Breakouts, stop loss placement, and breaches of important support and resistance levels are often driven by institutional trading behavior.

### Characteristics of Institutional Trading Behavior:

- **Price Breakouts**: Institutions typically buy or sell with large amounts of capital, causing price to break through key support or resistance levels.

---

## 2. Following Institutions: Strong Signal Bars and Favorable Context

To successfully follow institutional trading moves, you first need to identify **strong signal bars** in the market, then decide whether to enter based on the market context.

### Strong Signal Bars

Signal bars are bars that emit trading signals. These bars are characterized by relatively large bodies that significantly cover the previous bar's high or low, thereby emitting strong buy or sell signals. Strong signal bars typically appear as bull or bear bars, but their strength depends on body length and price change.

- **Bull bar body covering the previous bar's high**: This signal typically indicates a shift in market sentiment, suggesting strong buying pressure and a potential continued rally.
- **Full-body bull bar (no upper tail)**: This usually means selling pressure is insufficient, indicating weak bear strength and a potential bounce.
- **Full-body bull bar (no lower tail)**: This typically means buying is urgent, suggesting strong upward momentum that may persist.

### Favorable Context

Favorable context refers to the overall market trend and pattern, particularly the trend signals that appear during price corrections or pullbacks. Understanding the market context helps us judge whether to continue following the trend or prepare for a reversal trade.

#### Characteristics of Favorable Context:

- **Bull trend**: Consecutive **Higher Highs (HH)** and **Higher Lows (HL)** — price keeps making new highs, and each pullback forms a higher low.
- **Bear trend**: Consecutive **Lower Highs (LH)** and **Lower Lows (LL)** — price keeps making new lows, and each bounce fails to break the prior high.

#### Pullback Magnitude and Healthy Corrections:

- **Healthy corrections in a bull trend**: If the pullback magnitude is less than 50% of the prior leg's high without destroying the **Higher Low (HL)** structure, this constitutes a **high-probability buying context**.

#### EMA 20 Moving Average:

- In a **bull trend**, if price stays consistently above the **EMA 20** for 15 consecutive bars, this indicates strong buying force and a high probability of trend continuation.

---

## 3. Trade Structures for Following Institutions: Breakout Retests and Trend Continuation

After capturing strong signal bars and favorable context, traders can construct trade structures synchronized with institutions. Common trade structures include breakout retests and trend continuation.
