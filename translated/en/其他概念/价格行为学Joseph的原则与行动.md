# Price Action: Joseph's Principles and Actions

Joseph Imbornone's trading method is primarily built on Al Brooks' Price Action theory, combined with his personal practical experience and understanding of market microstructure. His core logic — which we are very familiar with — is to identify the current market cycle and use the Trader's Equation composed of probability, risk, and reward to execute trades. He has currently published 79 Emini review videos on YouTube. Let's summarize his trading principles for Emini and their corresponding actions.

## 1. Core Market Analysis Framework

### A. Market Cycle

Joseph believes the market always evolves in a specific sequence, and identifying the current phase is critical:

- **Spike (Breakout):** The market is in its strongest trend phase.
  - Criteria: Massive trend bars appear with closes near extremes, almost no overlap, leaving micro gaps.
  - Action: At this point, you can enter at market price or buy/sell on any small pullback, expecting a second leg.

- **Channel:** The trend weakens; two-sided trading begins.
  - Criteria: Pullbacks appear, but highs/lows continue to rise/fall.  Bulls begin taking profits at new highs; bears begin attempting counter-trend trades.
  - Action: Switch to a "buy low, sell high" approach (in the trend direction). Take profits at the channel line, expecting the channel to eventually evolve into a trading range and retrace to the channel's starting point.

- **Trading Range:** The market loses direction; bulls and bears are in balance.
  - Criteria: "Big up, big down" moves appear, bars overlap heavily, and breakout attempts fail (80% failure rate).
  - Action: Buy low, sell high. Buy at the bottom of the range, sell at the top. Avoid trading in the middle of the range; avoid chasing at the range edges.

### B. Gaps and the Open

- **A gap is a breakout:** Treat a gap as a massive invisible trend bar on the chart. If the gap is large, it usually means there will be a second leg continuing in the gap direction.

- **Bar 1 Rule:** The probability that the first bar becomes the day's high or low is only 20%. If the open shows strong trend, don't rush to trade counter-trend at the extreme, but don't blindly chase either — unless the breakout is very strong.

- **Globex Reference:** If the RTH (Regular Trading Hours) gap is small, it means overnight was a trading range; if the gap is large, check the 15-minute Globex chart for background structures like wedges or channel endings.

## 2. Specific Entry Strategies and Criteria

### A. Breakout & Second Leg

This is Joseph's most core strategy. Strong breakouts usually have follow-through.

- Criteria:
  - Strong trend bars (large bodies, small tails).
  - Follow-through bar: A same-direction strong bar immediately after the breakout bar, confirming the trend.
  - Leg reset: A strong breakout resets the leg count, treated as a new first leg (Leg 1).

- Action: Enter at the breakout or follow-through bar's closing price, or enter on the first pullback. Expect at least a second leg to test the extreme.

### B. Wedge / Three Pushes

After the market pushes three times in the trend direction, momentum often exhausts.

- Criteria: A clear three-push structure (1-2-3). If the third push breaks the channel line (parabolic climax), the reversal probability is higher.

- Action:
  - With the trend: Take profit.
  - Counter-trend: Wait for a strong reversal signal bar before entering, expecting a two-legged pullback (TBTL — Ten Bars, Two Legs), meaning approximately 10 bars or a two-leg structure pullback.

### C. Trap Trades

Profit from trapped traders' pain.

- **Second Leg Trap:** In a trading range or weak trend, the second leg push appears strong but is actually a bull/bear trap.
  - Identification: A strong bull bar (Leg 2) appears at the top of a trading range, but the context doesn't support a breakout.
  - Action: Trade the opposite direction (fade the breakout).

- **Limit Order Entries:** "A bad stop order is a good limit order."
  - Identification: At the bottom of a trading range, you see a bad sell signal (like a doji or weak bear bar); selling at this point is low probability.
  - Action: Place a buy limit order below that bar, betting on a failed breakout.

### D. High/Low 1, 2, 3

- Identification: Pullbacks in a trend. For example, in a bull trend, the first pullback high is H1, the second is H2.

- Action:
  - In a strong trend: H1 can be bought.
  - In a trading range or weak trend: Wait for H2 (double bottom) or H3 (wedge) before entering to improve win rate.
  - Trap: Don't buy H1 or H2 at the top of a trading range.

## 3. Key Supplementary Tools

### A. 10+2 Strength Scoring Factors

Joseph references Ali's system to quantify the strength of a trend leg, using it to determine whether the market is in a "trend" or "trading range."

- Factors include:
  - Bar body size (uniform and large).
  - Close position (near extremes).
  - Degree of overlap (the less, the better).
  - Micro gaps (present = strong).
  - Look not only at bars but also at whether they broke through resistance levels.

- Application: If the score is high, expect a second leg — buy pullbacks; if the score is low (heavy overlap, long tails), expect a trading range — buy low, sell high.

### B. EMA 20

- **Magnet effect:** When price is far from the moving average, it usually "reverts to the mean," especially on trading range days.
- **Trend confirmation:** In a strong trend, price stays on one side of the moving average for a long time. The first test of the moving average is usually a buy/sell opportunity.

### C. Always In

Binary assessment: Are bulls or bears currently in control?

- Identification: Consecutive strong bars, breaking important moving averages or trend lines, breaking prior highs/lows.

- Action:
  - If Always In Long: Only go long or stay flat. Can buy at market price, buy pullbacks.
  - If a Give Up Bar appears (a large counter-trend bar signaling capitulation): Exit the original direction immediately.

## 4. Risk Management and Exit Principles

- **Trader's Equation:**
  - High win rate = poor risk/reward ratio: For example, chasing a strong breakout (stop loss is far, reward may be limited, but win rate is high).
  - Excellent risk/reward ratio = low win rate: For example, picking a top at the range boundary (risk is small, reward is large, but win rate is low — possibly only 40%).
  - Principle: You must be clear about which type of trade you're making.

- **Stop Loss and Scaling In:**
  - In trading range markets, use wide stop losses and be prepared to scale in at better prices, aiming to lower the average cost and exit at breakeven.
  - Never scale in against a strong one-sided trend.

- **Default Exit:**
  - In a with-trend trade, if a strong counter-trend bar appears (such as a bear bar closing at its low in a bull trend), don't hesitate — exit immediately.

- **Time Management:**
  - Bar 75 Rule: Near the close (after bar 75, approximately 30-45 minutes before the close), don't open new positions. Only manage exits, because institutions are settling at this time and price action becomes unpredictable.
