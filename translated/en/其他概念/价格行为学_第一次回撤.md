# Price Action: The First Pullback (Retracement)

In any trend, the first pullback is often one of the highest win-rate, lowest-risk with-trend trading opportunities. The reason is that after the first pullback, the trend usually continues for at least one more leg, which provides an excellent risk/reward ratio for entry.

Characteristics of the first pullback:
It occurs shortly after the trend is established, usually following an obvious breakout leg.
The pullback magnitude is shallow — generally no more than half of the breakout leg.
A clear signal bar often appears at the end of the pullback.

The essence of the first pullback is a brief round of profit-taking after the breakout. Trend participants still dominate during this phase, and counter-trend forces are insufficient to reverse the trend. Therefore, as long as the context confirms the trend direction, the signal bar at the first pullback typically leads to rapid with-trend continuation.

Trading strategy:
Confirm the trend is established — at least one clear trend bar or a short trend channel.
The breakout direction is consistent with the higher timeframe trend.
Wait for the end-of-pullback signal to appear.
Close position is near the end in the trend direction.
Enter with the trend when the signal bar triggers.
Place the stop loss beyond the pullback low (for longs) or high (for shorts).
Position size can be somewhat larger since the win rate is higher.

Important notes:
If the first pullback is too deep, even breaking below the breakout leg's starting point, be alert — this could be a false breakout.
If the context shows opposing signals (such as higher timeframe resistance or support being broken), the quality of the first pullback diminishes.
In trading range markets, the so-called "first pullback" may just be a probe at the range boundary, and its success rate drops noticeably.

Extended opportunities:
Some trends produce a second or even third pullback after the first, but these pullbacks typically have lower win rates than the first. Professional traders prefer to focus on the first pullback and execute decisively when it appears, rather than chasing late in the trend.

The first pullback is a trading opportunity where "context and pattern are highly aligned." It combines early-trend momentum with an ideal risk/reward ratio, making it one of the structures most worth prioritizing in any with-trend trading system.
