# If You've Been Losing Money Long-Term

Many people keep losing money, but technique isn't actually the problem.
**The problem comes down to two things:**

1. Wrong entry points
2. Wrong stop loss methods

Put simply: **chasing highs to buy and lows to sell, while setting very tight stop losses** — it looks like "risk control" but is actually waiting for the market to sweep you out.

Once a loss occurs, fear sets in, and then:
Buying higher and higher, with tighter and tighter stops.
Selling lower and lower, with stops still very tight.
This kind of trading is **voluntarily feeding yourself to the market**.

You need to understand: **if you chase and buy high, you must pair it with a wide stop loss**.
**The same applies when chasing and shorting low**.
Otherwise, you're just making it easy for the market to wash you out.

The first thing you should do is: **reduce your position size**,
so you can tolerate a "reasonable" stop loss range.

---

**How to set a "reasonable stop loss"?**

For example, you buy above bar 35.
The truly reasonable stop loss is placed **below bar 18**.
Why?
Because **that is the starting point of the entire structure**.

Accept this stop loss distance, then enter with the **smallest position size**,
and do just one thing:

- If there's a pullback, see if there's a bull bar confirmation (not one of those with a long upper tail)
- Once confirmed (for example, above bar 52), add one more position

Once the market rallies, you have two choices:

- Close everything, safely lock in profits
- Close the upper position first, keep the lower one running for profits

**Enter with clarity, hold with discipline, exit with precision** — that's real trading.

---

**How to Rebuild Confidence? Start "Small and Simple"**

If you're currently suffering severe losses or your mindset has been shattered,
don't think about turning it around all at once. Take these three steps first:

1. Reduce your position to the **absolute minimum** — small enough that you don't care
2. Know exactly where your stop loss is — for example, below bar 18 as mentioned above
3. Don't blindly add to positions unless you see bull bar confirmation

Adding positions also needs justification, such as:

- A second entry signal
- A clear structural bull bar
- A significant low confirmation

Setting targets is also simple:

- First position: exit at breakeven
- Second position: ride for profits

**The benefits of this approach:**

- Higher probability
- Steady rhythm
- Mindset doesn't collapse
- Clear structure, consistent logic

For a trader who has been losing long-term, **this is a way to get back on track**.

---

**Summary in one sentence:**

It's not that you can't trade.
It's that you don't know where to trade or how to defend.
Get entry selection and management right,
and you'll no longer be at the bottom of the market's food chain.

Truly, technique was never the key.
**Surviving is.**
