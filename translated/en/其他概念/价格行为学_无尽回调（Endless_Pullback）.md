# Price Action: Endless Pullback

## I. Reversal Signals in Tight Channels

When you see a **very tight channel** with extremely shallow pullbacks, be especially cautious:

- Each pullback lasts only one or two bars;
- This type of structure reduces the probability of a strong bull continuation;
- It increases the likelihood of a "false breakout," trading range continuation, or even reversal.

---

## II. Probability Assessment for Tight Flag Breakouts

The probability of a **bear breakout from a bull flag** is typically **40%**.

But:

- **Once the channel persists for more than 20 bars**;
- And it's a very tight structure;
- The probability of a bear breakout rises to **close to 50%**.

What to look for:

- Whether there's a **large bear bar** as a breakout signal;
- Whether there's a **follow-through bar** for confirmation.

If both conditions are met:

At least **several bars of sideways or downward movement** can be expected.

---

## III. Endless Pullback and Bull Breakout

When the pullback structure keeps growing in bar count:

- The probability of trend resumption decreases;
- The probability of trend reversal (upward) increases.

When you see:

- The pullback has lasted more than 20 bars;
- It's a **very tight channel**;

Then you should start considering:

**This is very likely an "Endless Pullback."**

If the market then gives a strong bear breakout + follow-through bar:

There will most likely be a **second leg down**.

---

## IV. How to Handle Complex Pullback Structures?

Sometimes pullback structures are very complex and hard to judge:

- 50 or even 100 consecutive bars;
- Constantly debating whether it's High 1, High 2, or High 4.

The most direct approach is:

**Switch to a higher timeframe chart**:

- What was a chaotic structure may be a **clear High 2 flag** on the higher timeframe.

The same logic applies in reverse.

---

## V. High 2 Is the Golden Buy Point in Strong Trends

In a **clear bull trend**:

- If you're short, you may survive the market's first upward bounce;
- But when the **High 2 structure** appears, you **must exit and take the loss**.
- Bears try their luck after the first failure;
- On the second up move, **they capitulate and cover their shorts**;
- The bulls know this too and **choose to buy at High 2**.

At this point:

- **No one in the market is willing to sell**;
- Price must move up to find sellers, pushing price higher.

---

The tighter the channel and the more pullbacks, the higher the risk; the "Endless Pullback" is the precursor to a trend ending.
