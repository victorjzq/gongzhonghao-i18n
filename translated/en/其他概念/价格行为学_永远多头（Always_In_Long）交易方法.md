# Price Action: Always In Long Trading Method

## I. What Is "Always In Long"?

The "Always In Long" determination means: before price breaks below the low of this large bull bar, it is more likely to continue rising.

## II. Consecutive Bull Bars, With One Being Strong

When two consecutive bull bars appear on the chart and meet the following conditions:

- At least one of the bull bars has a relatively large body;
- And that bull bar closes near its high.

In this case, the market has entered an "Always In Long" state.

Specific confirmation method:

- The strong bull bar can be either the first or second bar;
- Price breaking above the second bull bar's high confirms the long signal.

## III. Special Trading Rules for "Surprise Bars"

When the market produces an unusually large bull bar that is significantly bigger than surrounding bars, we call it a "Surprise Bar." The following trading strategy can be applied:

- Buy at the 50% pullback (PB) level of this large bull bar;
- Or wait for price to retrace and then buy on the resumption of the rally.
- If subsequent bars have no bear bodies, buy the follow-through directly.

- If the "Surprise Bar" is very large and notably prominent, there's approximately a 70% probability that the market will achieve at least one measured move target upward (MM up);

## IV. Small Pullback Bull Trend

A small pullback trend is a bull-dominated rising structure where pullbacks are small in magnitude and short in duration, typically lasting only 1 to 3 bars — a manifestation of strong trend.

- Common pullback patterns include: High 1, High 2, and wedge bull flags;
- When a pullback (PB) or consolidation (such as an inside bar) occurs, traders choose to go long again above a bull bar closing near its high;
- Even if some "Always In Long" traders exit mid-way, as long as the trend maintains its AIL state, they will re-enter during flag structures.

Note: The market context ideally should be weak decline or sideways. This makes it easier to enter Always In Long mode.

Mastering the core logic of "Always In Long" and "Surprise Bars" will help you judge price action more clearly and improve your trading success rate.
