# Price Action: The Goal of Bar-by-Bar Review

Traders should use an insignificant position size and establish bar-by-bar review goals

to help themselves stay calm during trading,

and practice subconscious recognition while accumulating chart patterns and information.

When trading, you should be as calm as you are when reviewing.
The mental state you have while doing chart annotation or review
is the state you should pursue while trading.

And the only way to achieve this
is by using an "insignificant position size,"
with no external pressure,
where you can focus,
without distractions,
just reading bar by bar with attention, living in the present.
There should be no difference between live trading and reviewing.

There will always be doubters.
Perhaps everyone starts as a doubter.
I doubted for several years myself.
It's really hard to believe someone can look at charts,
understand the meaning conveyed by every single bar,
and have a system to trade it that is consistently profitable and provides peace of mind.

But at some point,
you must sincerely believe this is possible.
Otherwise, you can never succeed.
If you keep believing it's impossible,
you'll be stuck forever.

But with time, continued learning and understanding,
this belief forms naturally.
You develop knowledge,
you cultivate confidence,
because you truly see things in the charts that you can understand.
You see patterns,
you see the market's rational behavior.
And you see it over and over again.

You need to look at many charts.
This isn't something where you suddenly "get it" one day —
it builds up little by little, every day.

There will always be people who don't believe it's possible,
or they'll tell you "you'll never make it" or "you can't do it."

If you're someone easily influenced,
you must have psychological resilience to block out these voices.
Let me emphasize again: this takes time.
I believe that if a person persists long enough,
success is inevitable.

As long as you keep going and don't give up,
failure is simply impossible.
You just need to treat every setback as a learning opportunity.
Every adversity is an investment in becoming stronger.
