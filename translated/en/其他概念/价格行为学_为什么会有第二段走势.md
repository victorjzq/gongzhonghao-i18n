# Price Action: Why There Is a Second Leg

Regardless of the context, regardless of the leg count,
as long as a "three-bar bull micro channel" appears,
if you buy at the close of the third bar,
there is roughly an 80% probability
that you can exit at breakeven or better before your stop loss is hit.

In other words, if you buy at the close of the third bull bar
and scale in during the pullback,
you can avoid a loss.

When we say "disappointing,"
what we actually mean is: traders are trapped.

So what are "trapped traders"?
These traders can be roughly divided into three categories,
and they are the core driving force behind the market's second leg.
This happens in both rallies and selloffs.

First, in a rally,
some traders are trapped below.

After a consecutive upward breakout, bears accumulate losses,
and some bulls missed part of the rally.

This dynamic drives the second leg up.
Because the trapped bears are holding losing positions,
they will look for opportunities to exit during a bounce to reduce their losses.
Meanwhile, the bulls who missed the breakout will look for a pullback to enter.

Once the market pulls back, both bulls and bears buy together, forming the second leg up.

Let me put it another way:
The first type of trapped traders are the bears — the order doesn't matter —
wherever they are, they got trapped after a breakout.

That means they are now holding losing positions
and are eager to reduce their losses or exit at breakeven on any pullback.

The second type of trapped traders are the bulls who missed the rally.
They saw the breakout bar
but didn't dare to buy high, so they missed the move.
Now they're hoping to enter on a pullback.

The third type are the bulls who already bought in.
When the trend started, the win rate wasn't yet clear,
so they only bought a small position.
After the move went in their favor as expected,
they want to add to their position on a pullback,
scaling in up to their account's maximum risk tolerance.

These three types of traders —
trapped bears, bulls who missed the opportunity, and bulls wanting to scale in —
their collective demand drives the second leg.

Three-bar micro channel:
buy at the close, scale in on the pullback.

If the pullback reaches two-thirds of the swing,
then the probability of trend continuation drops significantly.

You can consider exiting outright,
or exiting near the original entry point,
which is the close of the third bar.
