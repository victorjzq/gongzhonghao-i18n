# Price Action: Breakout Follow-Through

When observing charts, if there are two bars and the second bar's body is larger, the probability of a pullback increases accordingly.

The pullback usually occurs within the next bar or the next few bars.

Regarding reversal plus follow-through patterns: if the follow-through bar has a large body, there is an extremely high probability that the market will first pull back.

The definition of breakout plus follow-through is: after the breakout bar, the follow-through bar's close must exceed the range of the first bar. That is, for a bull pattern it must close above the high, and for a bear pattern it must close below the low.

The first bar has a large body, while the follow-through bar has a smaller body but is still strong. The follow-through bar typically closes near the high, with a clear body — it is a trend bar, not a doji or trading range bar.

The first bar has a greater impact. When the market is searching for direction, seeing a trend bar creates a sense of pressure and risk. Once a follow-through bar appears alongside it, the situation becomes urgent, forcing traders to manage their positions.

A breakout pattern with follow-through, if most of its range is still below the moving average, has a higher probability of weak subsequent follow-through, and upside potential is also limited.
