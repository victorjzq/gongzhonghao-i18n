# The Key to Improving Trading Win Rate: Understanding Legs, With-Trend and Reversal Strategies

In the trading market, **Leg** is an important concept that helps us judge market movements, identify trend direction, and guide us in making with-trend or reversal trades. This article will provide a detailed analysis of **the definition of Leg**, **how to use Legs to identify trend direction**, and **how to improve trading win rate**.

---

## 1. What Is a Leg?

**Leg** is a very important concept in trend analysis, referring to the smallest momentum unit of a trend. According to the definition of trends, a Leg manifests as a single-directional price movement, usually consisting of at least three bars in the same direction. This is the basic building block of swings and trends.

### Characteristics of a Leg:

* **Single-directional price movement**
  A Leg typically represents sustained price fluctuation in one direction.

* **Smallest momentum unit**
  Each Leg is a fundamental building block of market trends.

* **At least three bars**
  A Leg usually consists of at least three bars, showing a certain degree of momentum.

### The Role of Legs:

Legs help us identify market movements. Especially within larger trends, we predict subsequent market behavior by identifying the starting and ending points of Legs.

## 3. Narrow Channel Three-Push Strategy: Avoid Premature Reversal Trading

When no obvious counter-directional Leg has formed, the market often behaves as a narrow channel. At this point, we should not make reversal trades directly but instead wait for **the appearance of the first counter-trend Leg**. When price breaks through the counter-directional leg's starting point and fails, the market will most likely transition into range-bound trading, suitable for trading the range.

---

## 4. Recent Leg Analysis: Determining the Main Trend Direction

### Find the Two Most Recent Legs: Compare Their Sizes to Judge Trend Direction

To confirm the current main trend direction, we can compare the sizes of the two most recent Legs:

* **If the two Legs are roughly the same size**, the market is very likely in a **trading range**, with no clear directional price movement, making it difficult to determine a clear trend in the short term.

* **If the most recent Leg is significantly larger than the previous Leg**, the market is more likely to continue the trend. A large Leg indicates strong market momentum, and the trend is likely to persist.

### Trade in the Direction of the Leg: Win Rate Is Higher After Two Counter-Directional Failures

When trading in the direction of the most recent Leg, after **two counter-directional failures**, the win rate of with-trend trading increases significantly.
