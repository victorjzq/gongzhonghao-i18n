# Price Action: Double Tops and Double Bottoms (Part 1)

## Basic Definition

A double top or double bottom can be either a flag or continuation pattern, or it can become a reversal pattern. The key depends on scale, context, and subsequent momentum.

## Significance of Micro Structures

Micro double tops / micro double bottoms typically consist of 2-5 bars.

These structures themselves have small ranges, but when they appear at the second leg of a larger double top / double bottom, they significantly increase the overall reversal probability.

## Double Tops and Double Bottoms in Flags

Pullbacks in bear trends often form small bull trends; these small trends frequently end with a double top, forming a "double top bear flag."

Pullbacks in bull trends similarly form "double bottom bull flags."

When a pullback is fewer than 20 bars, it is more likely just a flag continuation; if it exceeds 20 bars, it transitions into a trading range, and reversal probability approaches continuation probability.

## Minor Reversals and Major Reversals

- Minor reversals: small in scale, few bars, often evolving into flags or trading ranges.
- Major reversals: require sufficient bar count and a clear double top / double bottom context. If the second reversal (High 2 or Low 2) succeeds, there is a 40% probability of entering the opposite trend; 60% probability of merely returning to a trading range or the original trend.

Successful trades require at least a 2R target to cover the 60% unsuccessful scenarios.

## Low Probability Trades and Money Management

Counter-trend traders who enter at every suspected double top / double bottom solely because "the risk is small" will accumulate many small losses, leading to death by a thousand cuts.

Low probability entries must be paired with swing trading targets; if one rushes to take small wins, the long-term mathematical expectation is negative.

## Triple Tops / Triple Bottoms and Nested Structures

Three pushes (or more) need not be perfectly equidistant:
- Triple bottoms: decline then bounce x3, showing bears' momentum is exhausted.
- Triple tops: rally then pullback x3, showing bulls' momentum is weakening.

Nested double tops / double bottoms (small structures embedded within large structures) further increase reversal credibility.

## Operational Tips

a. Identify the second entry: High 2 for longs, Low 2 for shorts — the success rate is higher than the first entry.
b. If the reversal is fewer than 20 bars, treat it mostly as a flag; prioritize trading with the trend.
c. After a reversal is established, two-leg measured move targets are common; manage positions starting at 2R.
d. After consecutive momentum bars (sell-the-close / buy-the-close), watch the opposite extreme — rapid counter-directional swings are likely.

## Conclusion

Double tops and double bottoms are not "perfect patterns" but rather the visible traces of repeated bull-bear probing. Understanding their scale, context, and the process of failure-and-retry is far more important than rigidly adhering to pattern names. Placing micro structures, flag continuations, major reversals, and trading range breakouts into the same framework is the key to building consistent and executable trading decisions across different scales.
