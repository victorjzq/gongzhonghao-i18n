# Price Action: Reversal Trading (Part 2)

## 1. Beginners Prefer Small Reversals but Ignore Probability and Reward

1. They have small accounts and fear losses, focusing only on reversal trades with "minimal risk," often losing in counter-trend trades.
2. Low risk often means low probability, making it hard to profit most of the time.
3. Accumulated small losses lead to death by a thousand cuts.
4. Low win-rate reversals cannot compensate for losses without the large profits of **swing trading**; but beginners rush to exit at the first small gain, dooming the strategy to failure.

## 2. Classification of Reversals and Market Outcomes

1. **Minor reversals**: usually trigger a trading range or flag consolidation; a new trend is hard to initiate.
2. **Major reversals**: require **at least 10 bars** to construct a major trend reversal structure before the direction can truly switch.
3. The starting point of almost all reversals is accompanied by a breakout, even if very small.

## 3. "Nested Patterns" Enhance Signals

1. A small pattern appears within the structure of a larger pattern of the same type — for example, a small wedge is the third push of a larger wedge.
2. When the same pattern can be drawn from multiple angles at the same location, the reversal probability is higher.
3. Most algorithms can identify at least one version; if multiple algorithms simultaneously generate signals, the reversal is more credible.

## 4. Market Cycles and Major Trend Reversals

1. Bear trend -> trading range -> bull trend: a bear market reversal needs to first enter a trading range to accumulate buying pressure.
2. Example: A bear trend evolves into a trading range, forming a double bottom or higher low + large bull bar closing near its high, indicating that buying pressure is accumulating, increasing the probability of a successful major reversal.
3. Reversals lacking momentum or pressure are only minor reversals.

## 5. Probability and Targets of Major Reversals

1. The success rate of major reversals is approximately 40%; the remaining 60% become trading ranges or continue the original trend.
2. After entering on a reversal, the **minimum target** is: 10 bars, two legs (Ten Bars, Two Legs).
3. Most major reversals are accompanied by double top/double bottom structures and can be treated as swing trades.
4. Since the success probability is only 40%, a **reward-to-risk ratio of at least 2:1** is required to achieve a positive mathematical expectation.

## 6. Entry Timing: Reversals vs. Trends

1. **Reversal entry**: Lowest risk, positioned at the bottom of a bull or top of a bear, but the success rate is low.
2. **Trend entry**: H2 bull flags, buying climax low tests, three to four downward pushes forming a wedge bottom, etc., are all entry opportunities for subsequent legs.

## 7. Trend Reversals Require Channel Breakouts

1. All reversals begin with a channel breakout:
   * Bear trend to bull: requires breaking above the upper rail of the bear channel;
   * Bull trend to bear: requires breaking below the bull channel and trend line.

2. After the initial breakout, to confirm the reversal, a **second reversal** must occur:
   * Bull to bear: break below the channel bottom and test the prior high, forming a lower high double top;
   * Bear to bull: break above the channel top and test the prior low, forming a higher low double bottom.

## 8. The Essence of Double Top/Double Bottom Structures

1. Regardless of the name (double top, wedge, failed flag), all major reversals are essentially double tops or double bottoms at their core: the two highs need not be exactly the same, but both represent failed bull attempts.
2. When identifying, focus on: the second high approaching the first failure level, accompanied by a clear reversal signal.

## 9. Practical Key Points

1. First determine whether the reversal is minor or major, then decide on position size and stop loss.
2. Set reasonable targets: based on leg 1 = leg 2, ten bars two legs, or breakout body height measured targets.

## Conclusion

Reversal trading requires both capturing the earliest entry opportunity and assessing structural strength and probability. Watch for trading ranges in minor reversals and pursue trend switches in major reversals. Combined with nested patterns and channel breakouts, you can achieve high win-rate swing trading with controlled risk.
