# Trading Success Studies: Reading Failure from a Dynamic Perspective

Late at night, just jotting down some thoughts and recording them in time.

1\. The titles of previous notes in this series were simply written as "Notes," which is not ideal — it makes it hard for me to look back at the content later. Going forward, I'll try to use some keywords as titles for the notes series.

2\. Whether it's the various interviews with great traders online or the Price Action and "The Art of War" series I've been writing recently, none of them actually address "what exactly should you do." This is actually a profound question. If we don't study specific methods and actually put them into practice, all the so-called mindset wisdom we learn becomes nothing but "trading success motivation" — ending in mental gratification without ever actually learning to trade. In popular terms, it means lacking the unity of knowledge and action. I encountered many such classmates during my school days — they loved reading books about study methods and talking about self-discipline, but their grades never improved. To understand specific methods, you need to watch the original courses from Al Brooks, watch the study group videos, and read Victorjia's articles. At the same time, daily practice of 2-3 trades and review are indispensable.

3\. Trading is "the art of reading failure" — this phrase deserves deep understanding. When we see a narrow channel, we default to expecting the reversal of the narrow channel to form a trading range. In this trading range, we still buy high and sell low, or just hold our positions. The probability of breakout in either direction from this trading range is 50%. Moreover, in the direction of the trend, after about 20 bars of sideways movement, there might be a slight edge for a downward breakout. In that case, there's no need to trade back and forth — identify a trend and enter, then hold until the market proves your trend thesis wrong.

4\. As a trading system with first-principles thinking, Price Action has unlimited potential. As long as there are bars, you can trade using Price Action. When you have time, set up accounts or certifications for more trading instruments — always be prepared.

5\. Reasonable position reduction is necessary; big moves are bonus rewards. Take partial profits at the close based on the situation; don't over-fantasize. Having learned MM (money management), I would only take profits at fixed positions, which is actually rigid and fails to practice "the art of reading failure." I have a hypothesis about why many people say they lose even more after watching Al Brooks' courses — I think one reason might be that when studying, all those bars are static, but in actual trading, bars are dynamic. The transition from learning to applying requires personal calibration.

6\. Every day has unlimited trading opportunities. Don't rush, don't rush, don't rush.

You must study Price Action.
