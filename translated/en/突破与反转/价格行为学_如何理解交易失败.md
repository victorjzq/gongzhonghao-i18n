# Price Action: How to Understand Trading Failures

Beginners are always mechanically searching for trading signals and patterns, overly fixated on specific trading signals. These concepts are inherently simple, but integrating all the content is precisely where the difficulty of trading lies. Focus more on the overall trading context. When price crosses to the other side of the moving average, the probability of a tight trading range forming is extremely high — the market easily forms a narrow trading range.

Sometimes the trading pressure can be enormous. When extremely strong bearish bars appear, maintaining a long position is truly challenging. Although you have not yet seen the complete chart development, what is in front of you is a series of extremely strong bearish bars. At this point, it is very difficult to believe that bulls can still hold, but upon reflection, you must wait until a clear bearish signal appears before panic-stopping out. As long as that signal has not appeared, you should continue to hold and observe.

Just as Al emphasizes in his trading livestreams when analyzing daily charts, sometimes swing trade outcomes are often disappointing. There is actually no very clear standard for defining the degree of this disappointment.

Everyone's stop loss criteria are different. You need to set your own stop loss conditions based on the characteristics of the trading instrument, with clear criteria for determining when a trade has failed. You must be very clear in your mind that whatever stop loss exit conditions you establish are just as important as any other trading decision. In most cases, these conditions will save your trade and prevent losses from expanding.

However, in some situations, a breakout test will fail, but the actual trading signal is still valid. Although you have already exited, you later discover that continuing to hold would have been better. There is no need to feel regret about this — it was expected all along. Trading success and failure have their own probabilities. Even the risk control settings of a strategy have corresponding failure probabilities. Some patterns that seem appropriate for exiting are later proven not to be the real exit points. But at the moment when a decision must be made immediately, you have all the information needed to decide. Since you have executed the exit, that is fine. You strictly followed your trading system to execute, and even if you discover after closing that your judgment was wrong, there is no need to worry. The key is to build a long-term effective trading system.

When trading the second reversal signal, especially after waiting for the first signal to be triggered, this strategy has a high win rate and sustainability. Sticking to your trading discipline is more important than agonizing over the gains or losses of a single trade. Know that you will eventually accumulate a large sample of trading data covering various trading behaviors and their corresponding probabilities. Each trading sample matters, but it is also not that critical. What is more important is the process that generates these samples — your trading strategy or operating system. Therefore, you must strictly execute your trading discipline. The key is to value the trading process rather than obsessing over single trade results. Profits and losses on individual trades are normal, but as long as the trading system withstands the test of time, it is like a positive expectancy strategy. Historical backtesting data has fully verified that as long as you strictly execute this methodology, you can achieve a 65%-70% win rate. By year-end settlement, you will certainly achieve stable profits. Therefore, the key is whether you can persist in executing the strategy, not worrying about daily gains and losses. The fluctuations of individual trades are all essential steps in the trading process, guiding you toward your ultimate goal. Strictly executing the trading process is the key — it is more valuable than obsessing over the profits or losses of a single trade.

Failure is the norm. Whatever you come up with will have a failure rate of at least 20%, and accepting this is important. It is a tool that enables you to trade.
