# Price Action: Key Details of Double Top and Double Bottom Reversal Structures

## 1. Double Bottom Structures Are Not Always Symmetrical

In real markets, **double bottoms or double tops are rarely perfectly symmetrical**.

For example:

* The context is a weaker bull trend;
* A "double bottom bull flag" appears;
* The second low is slightly lower than the first, but is still valid.

---

## 2. When You See a Double Top Bear Flag, Think About the "Measured Move Target"

When you see:

* A double top bear flag appearing in a **bear trend**;
* Or a double bottom bull flag appearing in a **bull trend**;

Immediately think: **This may develop into a "Measured Move" structure**.

---

## 3. How to Calculate Measured Targets (Measured Move from Double Tops)

Even if the two highs are not exactly the same price:

* Use the **lower of the two highs** for the measured calculation;
* When price breaks below the neckline, use the **higher high** to determine the new target.

Using a conservative approach to estimate risk and targets is more robust.

---

## 4. Be Especially Cautious of Strong Bounces in Wide Bear Channels

---

* Sometimes there will be very strong bounces;
* But as long as **the previous significant downswing high has not been broken**, caution is still needed.

Especially:

* The bounce ends with a **Buying Climax bar**;
* The market then pulls back, forming a lower high, which constitutes a **double top structure**.

At this point, you can go short below the bear bar. Even if you are currently "Always In Long," you must realize: **there is a 40% probability of downside development**.

When the market forms a double top and a downside break occurs:

* If accompanied by a **large bear bar** and follow-through bars;

  Many traders will choose to:

* **Go short at the bear bar's close**;
* Or **place a sell stop below the next bear bar**.

Typical scenario:

* Six to seven bull bars appear (most closing higher);
* But still unable to break above the previous downswing high;

The overall structure remains a **wide bear channel**.

This can be:

* A **double top bear flag**;
* Or a **Low 2 Bear Flag**.

Even if the win rate is not high, as long as the **stop loss distance is small and the structure is reasonable**, it is still a good trade.

---

## 5. Bounce Structure After a Selling Climax

---

* The market breaks down, reaching the **measured move target**;
* Bears begin to take profits, the market bounces but lacks strength;
* Then it drops again;
* Eventually a **true bottom** forms.

Bottoming signals:

* A double bottom structure appears;
* First low + second test;
* Forming a "double bottom."

---

## 6. Expanding Triangle and Complex Reversals

The structure is as follows:

* Market highs keep making higher highs;
* At the same time, lows keep making lower lows;
* Forming an **Expanding Triangle**;
* A buy signal and strong breakout appear.

If a double bottom structure then appears: in a bull trend, **continuing to buy** is a reasonable strategy.

Even if the structure is not perfect:

* Multiple interlocking bear bars appear;
* Or a bounce appears in a strong bear channel;

As long as buyers still exist, the market may produce another "double bottom bull flag."

---

## 7. Trend Reversals and Flag Structures — The Operation Is the Same

Sometimes you cannot tell whether it is:

* A **bull trend reversal**;
* Or a **bear flag**;

No need to agonize — the operation is the same:

* Place a **sell stop below a bear bar**;
* The target is: price will move down from the current level.

Different names, **same operational logic**.

---

## 8. High 4 and Low 4: The Essence of Consecutive Reversals

Almost all "complex reversal structures" are essentially variants of High 4 / Low 4.

For example:

* The market tries to form a bottom (Wedge Bull Flag) and fails;
* Strong downside break + follow-through bars;
* Indicates there will be a second leg down.

Then:

* A second bottom-building attempt;
* A new wedge or High 2 appears;

This kind of complex reversal structure has a higher win rate.

Understanding the structure is the key to distinguishing real opportunities from trend reversals and failed breakouts.
